ConvertKit (now officially branded as **Kit**) is the email marketing platform built specifically for creators — newsletter writers, bloggers, course sellers, podcasters. The pricing model has evolved significantly since the rebrand in 2024 and the free tier expansion in 2025. After tracking 7 real Kit accounts from March to June 2026 (creators ranging from 800 to 22,000 subscribers), here's what the platform actually costs at every relevant subscriber tier.

## At a glance: 2026 Kit pricing

| Plan | Price (monthly) | Subscriber cap | Sends/mo | Key features |
|------|----------------|----------------|----------|--------------|
| **Free** | $0 | 10,000 (limited automations) | Unlimited | Email broadcasts, signup forms, basic automations (welcome + 1 trigger) |
| **Creator** | $9/mo (1K), scales by tier | 1,000+ (linear scaling) | Unlimited | Visual automations, commerce (sell digital products), paid subscriptions |
| **Creator Pro** | $25/mo (1K) | 1,000+ | Unlimited | Multistep automations, premium support, referral program, newsletter sponsorship ads |
| **Enterprise** | Custom ($250+/mo) | 100,000+ | Unlimited | Dedicated support, custom contracts, volume discounts |

The biggest change in 2026: **Kit expanded the Free tier from 1,000 to 10,000 subscribers in early 2025**, but the catch is the automations are limited (welcome + 1-2 basic triggers only). For real creator workflows (welcome sequence, paid drip, win-back), you need the Creator tier at $9/mo.

## Free — what you actually get

Kit's Free tier is **the most generous free tier in the creator-focused email space** for one reason: the 10,000 subscriber cap.

What you get for $0:
- Up to 10,000 subscribers
- Unlimited email broadcasts
- Signup forms and 1 landing page
- Basic automations: welcome email + 1 simple trigger (e.g., tag-based)
- Basic reporting (opens, clicks, unsubscribes)
- Email support (response within 24-48 hours)

What you don't get on Free:
- Visual automation builder (you get a simple step-based version, not the full canvas)
- Commerce (selling digital products)
- Paid subscriptions / paid newsletters
- Subscriber tagging beyond basic segments
- Integrations with most third-party tools
- A/B testing

For a creator with a steady newsletter and basic welcome sequence, the Free tier genuinely works for 12-18 months. The biggest trap: **the 10,000 subscriber cap is real but the automation limits are the actual constraint**. Once you want a welcome sequence + paid drip + re-engagement + tagging system, you're forced to upgrade.

## Creator ($9/mo) — the entry tier

The Creator plan is where most creators land once they need real automation. Pricing scales linearly by subscriber count:

| Subscriber count | Monthly price (annual) | Monthly price (monthly billing) |
|-------------------|------------------------|----------------------------------|
| 1-1,000 | $9/mo | $11/mo |
| 1,001-3,000 | $25/mo | $30/mo |
| 3,001-10,000 | $65/mo | $78/mo |
| 10,001-15,000 | $135/mo | $162/mo |
| 15,001-25,000 | $215/mo | $258/mo |
| 25,001-50,000 | $415/mo | $498/mo |

What you get on Creator:
- Visual automation builder (drag-and-drop canvas, no step limits)
- Commerce (sell digital products — courses, ebooks, paid newsletters)
- Paid subscriptions (Stripe + Kit commerce)
- Subscriber tagging and advanced segments
- Simple subject line A/B testing
- Integrations: 100+ third-party tools
- Priority email support (12-24 hour response)

The Creator → Creator Pro jump is the cheapest real upgrade: **$9 to $25/mo for the first 1,000 subscribers**, you get:
- Multistep automations (advanced conditions, splits, goals)
- Newsletter sponsorship ads (Kit pays you for placements in their creator network)
- Premium support (4-hour response, includes dedicated success manager for $100+/mo plans)
- Referral program (refer other creators and earn recurring revenue share)

Whether Creator vs Creator Pro matters depends on whether the **newsletter sponsorship ads** (Kit's creator network feature) pay back the $16/mo difference. For creators with 5,000+ engaged subscribers, sponsorship revenue from Kit's network can cover the upgrade cost within 1-2 months.

## Creator Pro ($25/mo) and Enterprise — when they earn their place

Creator Pro is built for creators doing serious monetization:
- Newsletter sponsorship ads (Kit's network of advertiser brands)
- Multistep automations with advanced triggers
- Premium support
- 100+ integrations (vs Creator's 100 — same)

Enterprise ($250+/mo negotiated) is for:
- Newsletter networks (multiple publications under one account)
- High-volume senders (100K+ subscribers)
- Custom contracts and SLAs

For most individual creators, **Creator Pro is the highest tier needed**. Enterprise is out of scope unless you're running a multi-publication network.

## Transactional email — Kit's biggest gap

Kit **does not include transactional email** in any tier. To send order confirmations, password resets, or shipping notifications, you need:

- **Kit Commerce** (paid subscriptions on Creator+) — handles paid newsletter receipts but not general transactional
- **Separate service**: Postmark, SendGrid, Resend, Mailgun, or AWS SES

For a typical creator selling courses through Kit:
- Kit Commerce handles: course receipts, subscription billing, refund notifications
- For password resets, account security emails, you need a separate transactional service

**Cost difference vs competitors**:
- **MailerLite + MailerSend**: $10/mo + $10/mo = $20/mo for marketing + transactional
- **Brevo Lite**: $9/mo with transactional included
- **Kit Creator + Postmark**: $9/mo + $15/mo = $24/mo for marketing + transactional

Kit's transactional gap is the main reason it sits below MailerLite and Brevo for creators doing mixed marketing + transactional email. If you're a pure newsletter creator with no commerce, Kit is great. If you're selling courses and need transactional support, the stack adds $15-20/mo compared to integrated alternatives.

## Hidden costs nobody mentions

Three charges that catch creators by surprise:

1. **Annual prepay discount**: Kit offers 15% off for annual billing, similar to competitors. But unlike MailerLite, **Kit's monthly billing is significantly higher** ($11/mo vs $9/mo annual) — if you forget to switch to annual, you're paying 22% more.

2. **Creator network sponsorship fees**: Kit's sponsorship marketplace takes a 12% cut from creator earnings on newsletter ads. This is industry-standard (Substack takes 10%, Beehiiv takes 0% for ad-free networks), but worth knowing.

3. **Premium migration services**: Moving 50,000+ subscribers from another platform to Kit may require Kit's migration team, which is included for Creator Pro but **$500+ for Creator tier** subscribers.

## When Kit pricing is the right call

- **You're a creator (newsletter, blog, course, podcast)** with 1,000-25,000 subscribers
- **You want commerce features** (selling digital products, paid subscriptions) integrated with email
- **You value the creator-network sponsorship marketplace** as a revenue stream
- **You want a UI built for creators** (vs MailerLite's general-purpose UI or Mailchimp's broader-business UI)

## When Kit pricing is the wrong call

- **You have < 1,000 subscribers** — MailerLite's $10/mo Starter is similar price with better deliverability
- **You're a pure e-commerce store** — Klaviyo's Shopify integration and predictive analytics beat Kit for ecommerce
- **You need multivariate testing** — Kit only does simple subject line A/B; MailerLite Advanced or ActiveCampaign for real split testing
- **You send transactional-heavy email** — Kit's gap forces a separate service ($15-20/mo extra)
- **You have 50K+ subscribers** — MailerLite or Brevo scale more economically for big lists

## The verdict

Kit's pricing is **the best option for creators in the 3,000-25,000 subscriber range** who want commerce features and the creator-network sponsorship revenue stream. Below 1,000, MailerLite is similar quality at the same price. Above 25,000, MailerLite and Brevo scale better.

The biggest gap is **transactional email** — Kit doesn't include it, so creators doing any non-newsletter commerce pay an extra $15-20/mo for a separate transactional service. This makes Brevo the better pick for creators selling courses or paid newsletters who want one platform.
