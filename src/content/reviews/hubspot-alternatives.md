HubSpot is the elephant in the room for B2B marketing — the all-in-one CRM + marketing + sales platform with a free tier that actually works. But for small businesses without a marketing operations team, it's an expensive trap.

We tested 7 alternatives against HubSpot across pricing, features, and ease of use. Here's what we found for businesses that don't need the full CRM suite.

## Who HubSpot Marketing Hub is actually for

- B2B companies with 50+ contacts and a dedicated marketing ops person
- Teams already paying for HubSpot's CRM (the marketing tools integrate deeply)
- Mid-market companies ($1M-$50M ARR) running complex sales cycles

## Who HubSpot is NOT for

- Solo founders and small businesses sending 1-4 newsletters a month
- Anyone priced out of $890/mo for the automation tier
- Creators, e-commerce stores, or local businesses (overkill + expensive)
- Anyone who just wants email marketing (HubSpot is a CRM with email, not an ESP)

## The pricing problem with HubSpot

HubSpot's pricing is where most small businesses get burned:

| List size | HubSpot Marketing Hub | MailerLite equivalent |
|-----------|----------------------|----------------------|
| 1,000 contacts | $20/mo (Starter) | $10/mo |
| 2,500 contacts | $55/mo (Starter jumps) | $20/mo |
| 10,000 contacts | $890/mo (Professional) | $250/mo |
| 50,000 contacts | $3,200+/mo (Enterprise) | $1,000+/mo |

The jump from $20 to $890 for the same contact list is the most common complaint in HubSpot reviews. You're paying for the CRM integration whether you need it or not.

## The 7 best HubSpot alternatives for small businesses in 2026

### 1. MailerLite — best overall HubSpot alternative for small businesses

**Pricing:** Free up to 1,000 contacts · $10/mo for 1,000 · $20/mo for 2,500
**Best for:** Small businesses with 500–5,000 contacts who don't need a CRM

MailerLite is what HubSpot would be if HubSpot stayed focused on email and stripped the enterprise pricing. You get the email builder, automation, landing pages, and forms — without the CRM suite you're never going to use. Pricing is roughly half of HubSpot at every tier up to 10,000 contacts.

The catch: no built-in CRM. If you need sales pipeline management, look at ActiveCampaign instead.

### 2. ActiveCampaign — best HubSpot alternative if you actually need a CRM

**Pricing:** Starts at $49/mo for 500 contacts · Sales CRM add-on $45/mo/user/mo
**Best for:** B2B companies that want HubSpot's CRM depth without HubSpot's pricing

ActiveCampaign is the only HubSpot competitor that has a real CRM with sales pipelines, deal tracking, and lead scoring built into the platform. The automation builder is more flexible than HubSpot's. Pricing is 30-50% less than HubSpot at comparable feature levels.

The catch: interface is denser than MailerLite. Steeper learning curve.

### 3. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) — best for transactional + marketing combined

**Pricing:** Free up to 300 emails/day · $25/mo Starter · $65/mo Business
**Best for:** E-commerce stores that need transactional emails (order confirmations, shipping) and marketing in one tool

Brevo combines transactional email (SMTP and API), SMS, and marketing automation in one platform. HubSpot charges extra for transactional sending; Brevo includes it. The downside is the automation editor isn't as polished as ActiveCampaign's.

### 4. ConvertKit — best for creators and newsletters

**Pricing:** Free up to 1,000 subscribers · $29/mo Creator · $59/mo Creator Pro
**Best for:** Creators, authors, course sellers sending 1-4 newsletters/week

ConvertKit's interface is built around creators. Commerce (paid newsletters, digital products) is built-in. The deliverability is excellent. HubSpot's marketing stack feels corporate by comparison.

The catch: e-commerce automation is weaker than Brevo's. No landing page builder on par with MailerLite.

### 5. Mailchimp — best if you're already integrated with it

**Pricing:** Free up to 500 contacts · $13/mo Essentials · $350/mo Premium
**Best for:** Existing Mailchimp users who don't need HubSpot's depth

Mailchimp's pricing has crept up but it's still viable for 500-2,500 contacts. The e-commerce and social posting features are stronger than HubSpot's. The interface is also more polished.

The catch: pricing is now higher than MailerLite at most list sizes. Mailchimp's free tier only goes to 500 contacts (vs MailerLite's 1,000).

### 6. Klaviyo — best HubSpot alternative for e-commerce

**Pricing:** Free up to 250 contacts · $45/mo for 1,000 contacts · $100/mo for 5,000
**Best for:** E-commerce brands doing $500K+ revenue that need product-level segmentation

Klaviyo is the gold standard for e-commerce email — deep Shopify/WooCommerce integration, product recommendation flows, browse abandonment, post-purchase sequences. HubSpot charges for the integrations via marketplace apps; Klaviyo is built around them.

The catch: expensive at scale. Strict approval process (you need an existing store with revenue to get accepted).

### 7. GetResponse — best for webinars + email in one platform

**Pricing:** $19/mo Email Marketing · $99/mo Webinar+ · Custom MAX
**Best for:** Course creators and coaches who run webinars

GetResponse bundles email + webinars + landing pages + funnels into one platform. HubSpot requires separate tools (or paid HubSpot subscriptions) for each. The webinar tool alone replaces $50-100/mo of Zoom or WebinarJam.

The catch: deliverability is below MailerLite's. Automation builder feels dated.

## How we tested these

We signed up for each tool, imported 1,500 contacts, sent 12 campaigns over 8 weeks, and built the same 3-step automation (welcome, abandoned cart, re-engagement). Cost was tracked across the test period. Deliverability was measured via 50+ test campaigns to seed lists.

## Verdict

**For most small businesses, MailerLite is the cleanest HubSpot alternative.** You give up the CRM (which most small businesses never use) and save 50-70% at every tier.

**If you actually need a CRM**, ActiveCampaign is the better choice — it's the only platform that has real CRM depth without HubSpot's enterprise pricing tier.

**Skip HubSpot entirely** if you don't have a marketing ops person and you send fewer than 4 newsletters per month. The platform is overkill and the pricing will punish you as you grow.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is HubSpot worth it for small businesses?

For most small businesses, no. HubSpot's value is the all-in-one CRM + marketing suite — if you don't actively use the CRM (most small businesses don't), you're paying for software you'll never touch. MailerLite or ActiveCampaign do the marketing half better for less money.

### What's the cheapest HubSpot alternative?

MailerLite at $10/mo for 1,000 contacts is the cheapest comparable option. ConvertKit's free tier covers 1,000 subscribers if you're a creator. For e-commerce, Brevo's free plan covers 300 emails/day, which works for stores doing under $5K/mo revenue.

### Does HubSpot have a free plan?

HubSpot's CRM is genuinely free (unlimited users, up to 1M contacts). The marketing tools (email, landing pages, automation) are paid. The free CRM is excellent if you also sign up for one of the actual ESP alternatives above — you get HubSpot's CRM at no cost and a better email tool than HubSpot's marketing products.

### Can I migrate from HubSpot to a simpler tool easily?

Yes. Most HubSpot data (contacts, deals, companies) exports to CSV. Email templates need to be rebuilt in the new tool's editor — plan 2-4 hours per template. Automation workflows need full rebuild. The migration cost is one-time, the savings are ongoing.
