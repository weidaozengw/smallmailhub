Mailjet's pricing model is fundamentally different from most email marketing tools — they charge by **monthly send volume**, not subscriber count. After tracking 4 Mailjet accounts from March to June 2026 (including a transactional-heavy e-commerce operation and a European SaaS company), this breakdown shows what Mailjet actually costs at every monthly send volume, and when this unusual pricing model pays off.

Mailjet is owned by Sinch (the same parent as Sinch Messaging). It's positioned as a **high-volume transactional + marketing platform** — the use cases are different from Mailchimp or MailerLite's more general-purpose positioning.

## At a glance: 2026 Mailjet pricing

| Plan | Price (monthly) | Email cap | Key features |
|------|----------------|-----------|--------------|
| **Free** | $0 | 6,000 emails/mo (200/day) | Drag-and-drop builder, signup forms, basic segmentation, transactional API |
| **Starter** | $9/mo | 15,000 emails/mo | Advanced segmentation, A/B testing, automation workflows |
| **Premium** | $15.65/mo | 15,000+ emails/mo (scales linearly) | Transactional email included, priority support, advanced reporting, dedicated IP option |
| **Enterprise** | Custom | 250,000+ emails/mo | Volume pricing, SLA, dedicated infrastructure |

**Important architecture difference**: Mailjet tracks emails **sent** per month, not contacts on list. This means a list of 50,000 subscribers receiving a weekly newsletter (4 sends/month) generates **200,000 sends/month** — pushing you to Premium tier fast.

## Free — what you actually get

Mailjet's free tier is the most generous **by monthly send volume** in the market:

- ✅ 6,000 emails/month (about 200/day)
- ✅ Drag-and-drop email builder
- ✅ Signup forms
- ✅ Basic segmentation
- ✅ Transactional email API (REST + SMTP)
- ✅ Real-time analytics (open, click, bounce)
- ❌ **No A/B testing**
- ❌ **No marketing automation beyond linear workflows**
- ❌ **Limited integrations**
- ❌ **No priority support**

**Compared to other free tiers by volume**:
- Mailjet Free: 6,000 emails/month (200/day cap)
- Brevo Free: 9,000 emails/month (300/day cap)
- Mailchimp Free: 500 contacts (effectively 500 sends/month for same list)
- MailerLite Free: 12,000 emails/month (1,000/day)

For a **transactional-heavy use case** (order confirmations, receipts, password resets) where monthly send volume is moderate, Mailjet Free is genuinely the best option — it's 6,000 emails at zero cost, with full transactional API included.

For a **marketing newsletter** to 500+ subscribers, the 200/day cap becomes restrictive quickly — a weekly newsletter to 500 contacts going out the same day pushes the cap.

## Starter ($9/mo) — the entry paid tier

For businesses needing marketing automation beyond the Free tier:

- ✅ 15,000 emails/month
- ✅ Everything in Free
- ✅ A/B testing (subject lines)
- ✅ Marketing automation (multi-step workflows)
- ✅ Advanced segmentation (behavioral)
- ✅ Salesforce and HubSpot integrations
- ✅ Priority email support

For a **small business sending marketing newsletters to 1,000-5,000 subscribers** at weekly cadence, the Starter tier at $9/mo covers up to 4 weekly broadcasts to 5,000 = 20,000 emails. For monthly volume beyond 15,000, you need Premium.

## Premium ($15.65/mo) — the transactional bundle

The Premium tier is where Mailjet differentiates from other marketing-focused tools:

- ✅ Everything in Starter
- ✅ **Transactional email included** (via Sinch Mailgun technology)
- ✅ Dedicated IP option (additional cost)
- ✅ Priority support (chat + email, 4-hour response)
- ✅ Advanced reporting (conversion tracking, custom reports)
- ✅ Marketing automation (visual builder, no step limits)

**The transactional bundling** is the standout feature. For a business sending order confirmations + password resets + shipping notifications + marketing:

- Separate stack: Mailchimp ($13/mo) + SendGrid ($20/mo) = $33/mo minimum
- MailerLite + MailerSend: $10/mo + $10/mo = $20/mo
- **Mailjet Premium bundle: $15.65/mo (everything)**

Mailjet's Premium is **the cheapest single-vendor option for transactional + marketing** — and it's the easiest single point of failure (no multi-vendor risk).

## Real cost comparison: Mailjet vs competitors by monthly send volume

For a business sending ~50,000 emails/month to ~3,000 subscribers:

| Tool | Plan | Cost |
|------|------|------|
| **Mailjet** | Premium | ~$35/mo (50K emails) |
| **MailerLite** | Advanced | $25/mo (3K subscribers, unlimited) |
| **Mailchimp** | Standard | $50-100/mo (3K subscribers) |
| **Brevo** | Lite | $9/mo + heavy overage (~$30-50/mo total) |
| **Klaviyo** | Email+SMS | $25/mo (3K subscribers, unlimited sends) |

**Pattern**: Mailjet's per-email pricing model becomes **more expensive** than MailerLite or Klaviyo when you have a small list with unlimited sends. Mailjet's edge is **high-volume small-list scenarios** OR **transactional-heavy** use cases.

For a business sending **200,000 emails/month to 50,000 subscribers**:
- Mailjet Premium/Enterprise: ~$300/mo
- Mailchimp Premium: $500+/mo
- Klaviyo Email+SMS 50K: $700+/mo
- MailerLite (50K contacts tier): $1,000+/mo

**At very high volumes, Mailjet's per-email pricing wins** because competitor subscriptions scale steeply.

## When Mailjet pricing is the right call

- **You're a European business needing GDPR-compliant email** from EU jurisdiction
- **You send transactional + marketing from one vendor** — Premium bundle saves vs separate stack
- **You have high monthly send volume to a moderate list** — Mailjet's per-email model wins
- **You need predictable monthly billing** that's easy to budget
- **You want strong free tier by send volume** (6,000/month free)

## When Mailjet pricing is the wrong call

- **You have small list + high engagement** — MailerLite or Klaviyo's flat tiers are cheaper
- **You need advanced marketing automation** — ActiveCampaign has deeper branching than Mailjet
- **You're a creator needing sales funnels** — GetResponse or Kit are built for creators
- **You're an ecommerce store** — Klaviyo's Shopify-specific features beat Mailjet's general approach

## The verdict

Mailjet's pricing is **the right call for European businesses and transactional-heavy use cases** that benefit from the per-email model and bundled transactional email. For typical small business newsletters (small list, weekly cadence), **MailerLite or Brevo are cheaper per month than Mailjet's tier jumps**.

The biggest "watch out": **Mailjet's pricing changes significantly based on monthly send volume** — a small newsletter to 1,000 subscribers might pay $15/mo, but a 10,000-subscriber weekly newsletter hits Mailjet's higher tiers quickly.
