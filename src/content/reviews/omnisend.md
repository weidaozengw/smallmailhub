Omnisend launched in 2014 as a Mailchimp alternative built specifically for ecommerce. Where Mailchimp tries to serve every audience (B2B, creators, ecommerce, nonprofits), Omnisend focuses on one thing: helping ecommerce stores drive more revenue through email, SMS, and push notifications. After testing Omnisend as the primary email + SMS tool for two Shopify stores over four months (January-April 2026), we have strong opinions about where Omnisend shines and where it falls short.

## Who Omnisend is for

- Ecommerce stores on Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, Magento, or BigCommerce
- DTC (direct-to-consumer) brands that need email + SMS in one platform
- Store owners who want ecommerce-specific automation (cart abandonment, browse abandonment, post-purchase) without building from scratch
- Stores with 500-10,000 email contacts (Omnisend's sweet spot)

Omnisend is **not** for B2B service businesses, content creators, bloggers, or nonprofits — the ecommerce-only feature set won't serve those audiences well.

## How we tested

We tested Omnisend on two Shopify stores over four months:

**Store A**: Apparel brand, 3,200 email contacts, $25K-$40K monthly revenue, no prior email automation
**Store B**: Beauty/skincare brand, 8,500 email contacts, $80K-$120K monthly revenue, previously used Mailchimp

Key metrics we tracked:
- Revenue per email sent (RPE)
- Cart abandonment recovery rate
- Welcome series conversion rate
- Email + SMS combined ROI
- Time to set up automations (vs Mailchimp baseline)

We compared directly to Mailchimp ($20-80/mo range) and Klaviyo ($45-150/mo range), Omnisend's two main competitors in the ecommerce email space.

## What Omnisend does brilliantly

### 1. Ecommerce-native email builder

This is Omnisend's standout feature. The email editor includes a **product picker** that pulls live product data directly from your Shopify/WooCommerce store:

- Drag a "Product" block into your email
- Select from your store's catalog
- Product image, title, price, and link auto-populate
- Discount codes can be added with one click
- Real-time product recommendations based on browse/cart data

With Mailchimp, you have to manually copy-paste product images, write HTML, and update links. With Omnisend, it's drag-and-drop. For stores that send 5-10 campaigns per week, this saves 3-5 hours per week.

In our testing, Store A (the apparel brand) increased campaign output from 4 per month (with Mailchimp) to 14 per month (with Omnisend) because the product picker made it so much faster.

### 2. Pre-built ecommerce automations

Omnisend ships with 30+ pre-built automation workflows designed specifically for ecommerce:

- **Welcome series** (3-5 email series, customizable)
- **Abandoned cart** (3-step series with discount code option)
- **Browse abandonment** (product viewed but not added to cart)
- **Cross-sell** (post-purchase recommendations based on order)
- **Win-back** (lapsed customer re-engagement)
- **Shipping confirmation** (transactional + product recommendations)
- **Review request** (post-delivery review prompts)

These templates are production-ready out of the box. With Mailchimp, you start from a blank canvas. With Klaviyo, you start from templates but they're less ecommerce-specific.

**Time to set up abandoned cart automation**:
- Mailchimp: 4-6 hours (build from scratch)
- Klaviyo: 1-2 hours (template + customization)
- Omnisend: 30-45 minutes (template + minor customization)

For stores without dedicated email marketers, this is a massive time saver.

### 3. Email + SMS + push in one platform

Omnisend bundles email marketing, SMS marketing, and web push notifications in one platform. This is genuinely useful for ecommerce stores where the highest-converting channel varies by audience:

- **Email**: Standard newsletters, promotions, automation
- **SMS**: Time-sensitive offers, shipping updates, flash sales
- **Push notifications**: Browse abandonment, cart abandonment (browser-level)

Compare to using Mailchimp ($20-80/mo) + a separate SMS tool like Postscript ($50-300/mo) + a push notification tool like OneSignal (free-$99/mo). The combined cost is $70-400/mo. Omnisend's $16-59/mo replaces all three.

**In our testing on Store A**:
- Email drove 62% of revenue
- SMS drove 28% of revenue (despite higher per-message cost)
- Push notifications drove 10% of revenue

SMS particularly over-performed for time-sensitive campaigns (flash sales, shipping confirmations). Bundling these channels in one tool made cross-channel automation (e.g., email → SMS if no open within 24 hours) much easier to set up.

### 4. Native ecommerce integrations

Omnisend integrates natively with all major ecommerce platforms:

- **Shopify**: Deep sync including products, orders, customers, cart events, browse events
- **WooCommerce**: Deep sync via official plugin
- **BigCommerce**: Native integration
- **Magento**: Official extension
- **PrestaShop, OpenCart, Wix**: Available but less deep

The Shopify integration is particularly strong. We tested product sync (8,500 products on Store B), order events, customer profiles, and cart abandonment triggers — all worked flawlessly. Compare to Mailchimp's Shopify integration, which requires Zapier for cart abandonment and has shallow product sync.

### 5. A/B testing on all paid tiers

This is a quiet but meaningful advantage. Omnisend includes A/B testing (subject lines, content, send times) on the $16/mo Starter tier. Most competitors gate this to $80+/mo tiers:

- Mailchimp: A/B testing starts at $99/mo Standard
- Klaviyo: A/B testing included but limited to subject lines on Free tier
- Constant Contact: A/B testing only on $80/mo Premium tier

For stores that test 3-5 subject lines per week, A/B testing on the entry tier saves money and improves open rates incrementally.

## Where Omnisend falls short

### 1. Pricing escalates at higher contact counts

Omnisend's pricing is competitive at the entry tier ($16/mo vs MailerLite $10/mo, Mailchimp $20/mo) but ramps quickly:

| Contacts | Omnisend | MailerLite | Mailchimp | Klaviyo |
|----------|----------|------------|-----------|---------|
| 500 | Free | Free | Free | Free |
| 1,000 | $16/mo | $10/mo | $13/mo | $45/mo |
| 2,500 | $59/mo | $28/mo | $20/mo | $75/mo |
| 5,000 | $99/mo | $44/mo | $80/mo | $150/mo |
| 10,000 | $199/mo | $96/mo | $150/mo | $250/mo |

At 2,500+ contacts, Omnisend is significantly more expensive than MailerLite. The justification is the ecommerce-specific features (product picker, pre-built automations, SMS), but for stores with basic needs, MailerLite delivers email at 50% of the price.

**In our testing on Store B** (8,500 contacts), Omnisend cost $199/mo vs Mailchimp at $80/mo. The ecommerce features were valuable, but the price delta was $1,428/year — non-trivial for a small business.

### 2. SMS pay-per-message costs

Omnisend bundles SMS into the platform, but SMS is **pay-per-message** on top of the subscription:

- US/Canada SMS: $0.015 per message
- UK SMS: $0.04 per message
- Other countries: $0.03-0.08 per message

For Store A (3,200 contacts, ~2,500 SMS sent per month), monthly SMS cost was $37.50 on top of the $16/mo subscription = $53.50 total. For Store B (8,500 contacts, ~8,000 SMS sent per month), monthly SMS cost was $120 on top of $199/mo subscription = $319 total.

If you send 5,000+ SMS per month, a dedicated SMS tool like Postscript ($50-300/mo with bundled messages) may be cheaper.

### 3. Email deliverability is good but not best-in-class

Across 100+ test sends per store, Omnisend's inbox placement landed in the **88-92% range**:

- Omnisend: 88-92%
- Klaviyo: 91-95%
- MailerLite: 92-96%
- Mailchimp: 84-88%

Omnisend uses shared IP pools, and its deliverability reputation is mixed because lower-tier users send from the same pools. To improve deliverability, upgrade to a dedicated IP (Pro tier $59+/mo) or use a separate transactional email tool like SendGrid or Postmark for critical sends (order confirmations, shipping notifications).

### 4. Ecommerce-only feature set

If your business model isn't pure ecommerce, Omnisend will feel limiting:

- No B2B features (lead scoring, sales sequences, CRM)
- No content creator features (paid newsletters, courses, memberships)
- No event marketing features (registration, ticketing)
- No nonprofit features (donation forms)

Omnisend is purposefully focused. This is good if you're an ecommerce store. It's limiting if you have a mixed business model.

## How Omnisend compares

**vs Klaviyo** ([read our full Klaviyo review](/reviews/klaviyo)): Klaviyo has deeper data segmentation and slightly better deliverability, but is significantly more expensive at higher contact counts ($75-250/mo vs Omnisend's $59-199/mo). Klaviyo wins on data depth. Omnisend wins on ease of use and price.

**vs Mailchimp** ([read our full Mailchimp review](/reviews/mailchimp)): Mailchimp is more versatile (works for B2B, creators, nonprofits) but has weaker ecommerce-specific features. Omnisend wins on ecommerce. Mailchimp wins on versatility.

**vs ActiveCampaign** ([read our full ActiveCampaign review](/reviews/activecampaign)): ActiveCampaign is better for B2B and complex automations, but lacks ecommerce-specific product picker. Choose ActiveCampaign for B2B. Choose Omnisend for ecommerce.

**vs MailerLite** ([read our full MailerLite review](/reviews/mailerlite)): MailerLite is significantly cheaper but has no ecommerce-specific features. Omnisend wins on ecommerce. MailerLite wins on price.

## When Omnisend is worth it

Omnisend is worth the premium pricing in these scenarios:

1. **You're a Shopify/WooCommerce store** with 1,000-10,000 contacts and need ecommerce-specific automation
2. **You want email + SMS + push in one platform** to avoid juggling multiple tools
3. **You send frequent campaigns** (5-10 per month) and value the time savings from the product picker
4. **You have a non-technical team** that needs pre-built automation templates
5. **You're growing fast** and need A/B testing on the entry tier

## When to choose something else

If any of these apply, Omnisend is not the best fit:

- **You're on a tight budget** at 5,000+ contacts: MailerLite is 50% cheaper for basic email
- **You send 10,000+ SMS per month**: A dedicated SMS tool may be cheaper
- **You need deep data segmentation**: Klaviyo is the data powerhouse in this space
- **You have a non-ecommerce business**: Mailchimp or ActiveCampaign are more versatile
- **You want best-in-class deliverability**: Klaviyo or MailerLite outperform Omnisend

## Final verdict

Omnisend is the best-in-class ecommerce email tool for stores with 1,000-10,000 contacts. The product picker, pre-built automations, and bundled SMS make it the most ecommerce-focused option in 2026. For Shopify and WooCommerce stores, it's our top recommendation if you can justify the price.

But Omnisend is not for everyone. The pricing at 5,000+ contacts is significantly higher than MailerLite, and the ecommerce-only feature set means it won't serve mixed business models. If you're on a tight budget, have a non-ecommerce business, or send massive SMS volumes, you'll find better value elsewhere.

**Rating: 4.3/5**

Omnisend loses 0.7 points for pricing escalation at higher contact counts, pay-per-message SMS, and good-but-not-great deliverability. It gains points for the genuinely useful product picker, pre-built ecommerce automations, and bundled email + SMS + push in one platform.
