Constant Contact launched in 1995, making it one of the oldest email marketing tools still in active development. While Mailchimp, MailerLite, and ConvertKit dominate the conversation in 2026, Constant Contact still has 600,000+ customers and $1.4 billion in annual revenue. After testing Constant Contact as our primary email tool for a small nonprofit and a local retail business over 90 days, we have a nuanced view of where this old reliable tool fits in the modern landscape.

## Who Constant Contact is for

- Small businesses with non-technical owners who need an email tool they can learn in a weekend
- Nonprofits that need donation forms, event registration, and survey tools built into the email platform
- Event organizers who need ticketing and registration integration
- Local businesses (restaurants, salons, retail stores) with simple email needs
- Organizations that prioritize phone/chat support over self-service

Constant Contact is **not** for ecommerce stores (lacks deep Shopify/WooCommerce integration), B2B SaaS (lacks automation depth), or creators/bloggers (better tools exist at lower prices).

## What Constant Contact does well

### 1. Ease of use

This is Constant Contact's enduring strength. The interface is designed for people who don't want to learn a complex tool:

- **Email editor**: Drag-and-drop, 60+ templates, no HTML knowledge needed
- **Contact management**: Simple import from Excel/CSV, no field mapping wizard to figure out
- **List building**: Sign-up forms, text-to-join, social media lead ads — all built in
- **Reporting**: Plain-English summaries ("Your open rate was 28%, which is good for your industry")

In our testing, a non-technical nonprofit director sent her first campaign within 30 minutes of signing up. With Mailchimp or ActiveCampaign, the same person needed 2-3 hours to get oriented.

### 2. Unlimited email sends

This is a quiet advantage most reviewers miss. Constant Contact's pricing is based on **number of contacts**, not monthly email volume:

| Constant Contact tier | Contacts | Monthly emails |
|-----------------------|----------|----------------|
| Core ($12/mo) | 500 | Unlimited |
| Email Plus ($35/mo) | 1,000 | Unlimited |
| Premium ($80/mo) | 5,000 | Unlimited |

Compare this to MailerLite's free tier (1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month cap) or Brevo's free tier (unlimited contacts, 300 emails/day cap). If you have a small list but email frequently (weekly newsletters, event reminders, donation appeals), Constant Contact's unlimited sends are valuable.

### 3. Nonprofit-specific features

Constant Contact has invested heavily in the nonprofit market. Built-in features include:

- **Donation forms** that integrate with Stripe, PayPal, and Donorbox
- **Event registration and ticketing** with attendee management
- **Survey tools** for donor feedback, volunteer surveys, board polls
- **Segmented reporting** by donor type, event attendance, engagement level
- **Nonprofit pricing**: 20% discount on all tiers if you're a registered 501(c)(3)

No other email tool matches this combination. Mailchimp and ActiveCampaign require third-party integrations for donation forms and event management, adding cost and complexity.

### 4. Customer support

This is increasingly rare in 2026: Constant Contact offers phone, chat, and email support on **all paid tiers** — including free trial. Most competitors have phased out phone support in favor of chat-only or paid support tiers.

We tested phone support three times during our 90-day trial:
- Wait time: 4 minutes, 8 minutes, 12 minutes (acceptable)
- Quality: 8/10 (agents were knowledgeable, no scripted responses)
- Resolution: All three issues resolved on first call

For small business owners who value talking to a human, this is a meaningful differentiator.

## Where Constant Contact falls short

### 1. Pricing

Constant Contact is more expensive than MailerLite, Mailchimp, or Brevo for similar functionality:

| Tool | 1,000 contacts | 5,000 contacts |
|------|----------------|----------------|
| MailerLite | $10/mo (unlimited emails) | $44/mo |
| Mailchimp | $20/mo (10,000 sends) | $80/mo |
| Brevo | $18/mo (20,000 emails) | $49/mo |
| **Constant Contact** | **$35/mo (unlimited emails)** | **$80/mo** |

Constant Contact's pricing is justified by the unlimited emails and support quality, but it's a 2-3x premium over MailerLite. For small businesses watching every dollar, MailerLite delivers 80% of the functionality at 25-30% of the price.

### 2. Email automation depth

Constant Contact's automation features are basic compared to ActiveCampaign or HubSpot:

- **Welcome series**: 3-step series, basic triggers
- **Date-based**: Birthday, anniversary, membership renewal reminders
- **Engagement-based**: Re-engagement campaigns, win-back series
- **Behavioral**: Email opens, link clicks, page views

What's missing:
- Multi-step conditional workflows
- Lead scoring
- Webhook actions
- Custom event triggers
- Advanced branching logic

If you need ActiveCampaign-level automation depth, Constant Contact will frustrate you. If your automation needs are simple (welcome + birthday + re-engagement), Constant Contact works fine.

### 3. Modern UX lag

The Constant Contact interface is functional but feels dated compared to MailerLite, ConvertKit, or Kit:

- Email editor is solid but slower than competitors
- Reporting dashboards are less customizable
- Mobile app exists but is limited (can't build campaigns from mobile)
- No dark mode (a small thing, but reveals the platform's age)

For users under 35, this UX lag is noticeable. For users over 50 or non-technical users, the interface is actually easier than the more modern tools (which assume comfort with software).

### 4. Limited integrations

Constant Contact has 300+ integrations vs Mailchimp's 800+ or ActiveCampaign's 900+:

- **Ecommerce**: Shopify and WooCommerce work, but integration depth is shallow (no cart abandonment templates, no product recommendation blocks)
- **CRM**: Native Salesforce and HubSpot CRM sync, but no Pipedrive, no Zoho
- **Landing pages**: Constant Contact's landing page builder works but is basic
- **Zapier**: Yes, but limited triggers/actions compared to Mailchimp

If your tech stack relies on deep integrations, Constant Contact will feel limiting.

## Pricing details and considerations

Constant Contact offers a 14-day free trial (no credit card required). After the trial:

- **Core ($12/mo)**: 500 contacts, unlimited emails, basic automation, email support
- **Email Plus ($35/mo)**: 1,000 contacts, unlimited emails, advanced automation, A/B testing, surveys, polls
- **Premium ($80/mo)**: 5,000 contacts, unlimited emails, everything in Email Plus, dynamic content, advanced reporting

A few things to watch for:
- **Contact overages**: Charges $5-10/mo per 500 contacts over your tier limit
- **Nonprofit discount**: 20% off if you're a registered 501(c)(3) — call sales to apply
- **Annual prepay discount**: 15% off if you pay annually ($122/year on Core tier)
- **Hidden costs**: Constant Contact doesn't charge for support or basic features, but the tier upgrades (Email Plus, Premium) can sneak up on you

## When Constant Contact is worth it

Constant Contact is worth the premium pricing in these scenarios:

1. **You're a nonprofit** that needs donation forms, event registration, and surveys built in
2. **You value phone support** and want to talk to a human when you have questions
3. **You have a small list but email frequently** (unlimited sends save money over volume-capped tools)
4. **You're a non-technical user** who wants the easiest possible tool to learn
5. **You run events** and need ticketing + registration + email in one platform

## When to choose something else

If any of these apply, Constant Contact is not the best fit:

- **You're on a tight budget**: MailerLite ($10/mo) or Mailchimp ($13/mo) are cheaper
- **You need deep automation**: ActiveCampaign ($49/mo Plus) or HubSpot ($890/mo Professional)
- **You're an ecommerce store**: Klaviyo ($45/mo) or Mailchimp ($20/mo) have better Shopify integration
- **You're a creator or blogger**: ConvertKit ($9/mo) or MailerLite ($10/mo) are better suited
- **You want modern UX**: MailerLite or Kit feel more current than Constant Contact

## Final verdict

Constant Contact is the Honda Civic of email marketing — not flashy, not the cheapest, not the most powerful, but reliable, easy to use, and well-supported. For small businesses, nonprofits, and event organizers who value ease of use and human support over advanced features and low prices, Constant Contact remains a solid choice in 2026.

But Constant Contact is not for everyone. The pricing is 2-3x higher than MailerLite for similar features, and the automation depth lags ActiveCampaign by a wide margin. If you're watching your budget, need deep automation, or want modern UX, you'll find better value elsewhere.
