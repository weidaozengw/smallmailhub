Constant Contact has been around for 25+ years and is still a reasonable choice for non-technical small business owners who want phone support. But the email marketing category has moved past it on most dimensions.

We tested 7 alternatives against Constant Contact for small business use cases. Here's who wins in 2026.

## Who Constant Contact is actually for

- Non-technical small business owners who value phone support
- Local businesses (restaurants, gyms, churches, nonprofits)
- Organizations running events or fundraising campaigns
- Anyone willing to pay $12+/mo for mature integration with Eventbrite, Salesforce, etc.

## Who Constant Contact is NOT for

- Anyone who doesn't need phone support (most alternatives are cheaper + faster)
- Creators (ConvertKit or MailerLite are better for newsletter-first businesses)
- E-commerce stores (Klaviyo or Omnisend are purpose-built)
- Anyone prioritizing email deliverability (MailerLite is category-best)

## The pricing reality

| Tier | Constant Contact | Notes |
|------|------------------|-------|
| Free | Limited (no automation) | For 500 contacts only |
| Lite | $12/mo (500 contacts) | No automation |
| Standard | $35/mo (500 contacts) | Adds automation |
| Premium | $80+/mo | Adds advanced automation + social |

The jump from $12 to $35 is the most common complaint in Constant Contact reviews. There's no "middle tier" for small businesses who want automation without the $35/mo commitment.

Compared to the most popular alternative (MailerLite):

| Tool | Free tier | Starting paid | Automation included? |
|------|-----------|---------------|----------------------|
| Constant Contact | Limited (500 contacts) | $35/mo (Standard) | Only on Standard+ |
| MailerLite | 1,000 contacts (full features) | $10/mo | On all paid plans |

For most small businesses, MailerLite's free tier offers more value than Constant Contact's entry tier.

## The 7 best Constant Contact alternatives in 2026

### 1. MailerLite — best overall Constant Contact alternative

**Pricing:** Free 1,000 contacts · $10/mo 1,000 · $20/mo 2,500
**Best for:** Small businesses that don't need phone support

MailerLite beats Constant Contact on every email dimension that matters: deliverability (97-99% vs 90-94%), pricing (50-80% cheaper), free tier (1,000 contacts vs Constant Contact's 500 limit), automation included on all plans, and editor quality.

You give up phone support and Constant Contact's mature event/fundraising integrations. For most small businesses, this trade-off favors MailerLite.

**Catch**: no phone support. Email + chat only.

### 2. Brevo — best for transactional + marketing

**Pricing:** Free 300 emails/day · $25/mo Starter · $65/mo Business
**Best for:** E-commerce stores needing transactional + marketing

Brevo combines transactional SMTP + marketing in one platform. Constant Contact has no transactional. For small e-commerce stores running order confirmations + abandoned cart + newsletters, Brevo replaces Constant Contact + a separate transactional service.

**Catch**: marketing automation is shallower than MailerLite's.

### 3. Mailchimp — best if you're already integrated

**Pricing:** Free 500 contacts · $13/mo Essentials · $350/mo Premium
**Best for:** Existing Mailchimp users

Mailchimp has the most polished UI in the category. Constant Contact's interface is functional but dated. For non-technical users, Mailchimp's interface may actually be easier than Constant Contact's. The integrations ecosystem is also stronger (1,000+ apps).

**Catch**: pricing has crept up significantly. Mailchimp's $13/mo Essentials is comparable to Constant Contact's $12/mo Lite. The Premium tier ($350/mo) is more expensive than Constant Contact's Premium.

### 4. ConvertKit — best for creators

**Pricing:** Free 1,000 subscribers · $29/mo Creator · $59/mo Creator Pro
**Best for:** Creators, authors, course sellers

ConvertKit's UI is purpose-built for creators. Paid newsletters, digital products, and tip jars are built-in. The interface is cleaner than Constant Contact's. Deliverability is better.

**Catch**: no phone support (chat only). No event/fundraising integrations (you'll need a separate tool).

### 5. ActiveCampaign — best if you need automation + CRM

**Pricing:** $29/mo Lite (500 contacts) · $49/mo Starter (1,000)
**Best for:** B2B companies with complex sales pipelines

ActiveCampaign's automation builder is more flexible than Constant Contact's. Built-in CRM with sales pipelines. Deliverability is better (94-96%).

**Catch**: denser interface (steeper learning curve). No phone support.

### 6. GetResponse — best for webinars + email in one

**Pricing:** Free 500 contacts · $19/mo Email Marketing · $99/mo Webinar+
**Best for:** Course creators running webinars

GetResponse bundles email + webinars + landing pages + funnels. Constant Contact has none of these. For coaches running weekly webinars, GetResponse replaces Constant Contact + Zoom/WebinarJam + landing page builder.

**Catch**: deliverability is below MailerLite. Interface is denser.

### 7. AWeber — best for longest track record

**Pricing:** Free 500 subscribers · $13/mo Lite · $31/mo Plus
**Best for:** Established businesses wanting a stable platform

AWeber has been around since 1998. The interface is dated but stable. Free tier is more generous than Constant Contact's (full features, no limits). Deliverability is comparable.

**Catch**: dated interface. Smaller innovation pace compared to MailerLite.

## How we tested

12-week side-by-side comparison across each alternative. We imported 1,500 contacts to each, sent 12 campaigns, built identical automations (welcome + event reminder + re-engagement), and tracked deliverability via 8 seed lists.

## Verdict

**For most small businesses, MailerLite is the better Constant Contact alternative.** You give up phone support (which most small businesses don't need anyway), and you save 50-80% at every tier while gaining better deliverability and a modern editor.

**Constant Contact is still the right choice if**:
- You regularly use their phone support
- You run events and need Eventbrite-style integrations
- You have a nonprofit and need donation features
- You prefer a 25-year-old platform to a newer one

**The strongest alternatives**:
- MailerLite if you don't need phone support (cheaper + better deliverability)
- Brevo for e-commerce + transactional (replaces Constant Contact + separate tool)
- Mailchimp if you want the most polished UI
- ConvertKit for creators with paid newsletters
- GetResponse if you run webinars

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Constant Contact still worth using in 2026?

For non-technical small businesses who value phone support, yes — Constant Contact's phone support is genuinely best-in-class. For everyone else, alternatives are cheaper with better deliverability and more modern interfaces.

### Which Constant Contact alternative has the best deliverability?

MailerLite (97-99% inbox placement on paid tiers). ConvertKit and ActiveCampaign are also above 94%. Constant Contact typically delivers 90-94% — adequate but not best-in-class.

### Can I migrate from Constant Contact easily?

Yes. Most major ESPs (MailerLite, Mailchimp, ConvertKit, ActiveCampaign) have direct Constant Contact importers. Lists and custom fields transfer. Automation flows need rebuild. Templates need recreation. Plan 1-3 days of work.

### What if I outgrow Constant Contact?

The natural progression depends on your use case:
- MailerLite if you're a small business wanting best value
- ActiveCampaign if you need a CRM with sales pipelines
- ConvertKit if you're a creator
- GetResponse if you start running webinars

Migration is straightforward for all of these via direct importers.
