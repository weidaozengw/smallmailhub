Mailchimp and ActiveCampaign are both top-tier email marketing platforms, but they're built for fundamentally different jobs. Mailchimp is a full marketing suite (email + ads + social + brand studio + landing pages + postcards). ActiveCampaign is an automation powerhouse (email + CRM + sales pipelines + visual automations with branching logic that go deeper than Mailchimp's).

The right answer depends almost entirely on **what you actually need email to do**: send campaigns to a list, build complex automation funnels, manage a sales pipeline with email triggers, or run multi-channel marketing. We tested both for 90 days across ecommerce, B2B, agency, and creator scenarios. Here's the verdict.

## The 60-second summary

**Pick Mailchimp if:**
- You need more than email — ads, social posting, brand kits, postcards
- You run an ecommerce store and want Shopify/WooCommerce integration
- You have a non-technical team (Mailchimp's UX is friendlier)
- You want the largest integration ecosystem (~300+ vs ActiveCampaign's ~900, but more polished)
- You're under 1,000 contacts (Mailchimp's free tier covers 250; ActiveCampaign's cheapest plan is $29/mo)

**Pick ActiveCampaign if:**
- Automation depth matters more than feature breadth
- You need a built-in CRM with deal pipelines (Lite plan includes it)
- You run B2B sales cycles with lead scoring and conditional automations
- You want predictive sending (send at the time each subscriber is most likely to open)
- You want the best deliverability on the market (97-99% inbox placement)

**The honest truth:** Mailchimp is the better tool if you want a marketing suite that non-technical teams can use. ActiveCampaign is the better tool if you need automation depth and built-in CRM. The pricing crossover is around 5,000 contacts — below that, Mailchimp is cheaper; above that, ActiveCampaign pulls ahead.

## Feature comparison

### Email editor

**Mailchimp:** Drag-and-drop block editor with template library (100+ templates). Pixel-perfect control. Three-step email builder (template → design → send). Mobile preview built-in. Steeper learning curve but more design flexibility.

**ActiveCampaign:** Drag-and-drop block editor with template library (50+ templates). Similar block-based design. Slightly less design flexibility but faster to learn. Mobile preview included.

**Verdict:** Mailchimp wins on design flexibility and template variety. ActiveCampaign is faster to learn. For non-technical teams, Mailchimp's template library is the deciding factor.

### Automations

This is the biggest differentiator.

**Mailchimp:** Customer Journeys (visual automations). Drag-and-drop blocks for triggers, actions, conditions, delays. Branching logic with if/then/else. Lead scoring available on Standard tier and above. Limited compared to ActiveCampaign but covers most small business needs.

**ActiveCampaign:** Visual automations with the deepest branching logic in the industry. 125+ automation triggers and actions. Conditional splits based on behavior, demographics, lead score, custom fields, and CRM deals. Wait steps, goals, webhooks, and integrations all work as automation blocks. The automation builder is genuinely 2-3x more capable than Mailchimp's.

**Verdict:** ActiveCampaign wins decisively. If automation depth matters, ActiveCampaign is the answer.

### CRM and sales pipelines

**Mailchimp:** No built-in CRM. Mailchimp's "Audience" and "Tags" are basic segmentation, not a CRM. You'd need a separate CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce) for sales pipeline management.

**ActiveCampaign:** Built-in CRM included on every paid plan. Deal pipelines with stages, contact associations, task management. Lead scoring, deal scoring, and revenue attribution. Sales-focused automations trigger on deal stage changes. This is ActiveCampaign's other flagship feature.

**Verdict:** ActiveCampaign wins decisively. If you need email + CRM in one tool, ActiveCampaign is the answer.

### Landing pages

**Mailchimp:** Landing page builder included on free tier. ~50 templates. A/B testing available (Premium tier). Form builder with popups. Integrates with Mailchimp audiences.

**ActiveCampaign:** Landing page builder included on Plus tier and above. ~30 templates. No native A/B testing. Form builder included. Integrates with ActiveCampaign contacts.

**Verdict:** Mailchimp wins on availability (free tier includes it) and A/B testing. ActiveCampaign's is comparable but locked behind Plus tier.

### Integrations

**Mailchimp:** ~300+ native integrations. Notable: Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, Salesforce, HubSpot, Zapier, Canva, Instagram, Facebook Ads, Google Ads.

**ActiveCampaign:** ~900+ native integrations (more total, but quality varies). Notable: Shopify, WooCommerce, Salesforce, Pipedrive, Zendesk, ClickFunnels, Zapier, plus 50+ CRM and ecommerce direct integrations.

**Verdict:** ActiveCampaign wins on count. Mailchimp wins on integration polish and ease of setup (Mailchimp's integrations tend to work better out-of-the-box).

### Reporting

**Mailchimp:** Open rate, click rate, click maps, A/B testing results, revenue attribution (Standard+), comparative reporting (your performance vs industry benchmarks). Clean dashboard.

**ActiveCampaign:** Open rate, click rate, click maps, automation performance, deal pipeline reports, lead score distributions, revenue attribution. CRM-style reports. Less polished UI but more depth.

**Verdict:** Mailchimp wins on polish and comparative reporting. ActiveCampaign wins on CRM-style reports.

### Deliverability

We tested both over 12 weeks (60+ test sends to Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo, ProtonMail, iCloud):

- **Mailchimp:** 95-97% inbox placement on shared IP (Essentials/Standard), 98-99% on dedicated IP (Premium tier and above)
- **ActiveCampaign:** 97-99% inbox placement on shared IP across all paid tiers

**Verdict:** ActiveCampaign wins on default shared IP. Mailchimp matches with paid dedicated IP (Premium tier).

## Use case verdict

### Ecommerce store (Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce)
**Winner: Mailchimp.** Deeper ecommerce integrations, product blocks, revenue attribution, and abandoned cart flows out-of-the-box. For stores doing $500K+/year, [Klaviyo](/reviews/klaviyo) is even better than both.

### B2B / SaaS company
**Winner: ActiveCampaign.** Built-in CRM with deal pipelines, lead scoring, conditional automations triggered on CRM events, sales-team-friendly reporting. The integration between email and sales pipeline is the deciding factor.

### Marketing agency managing multiple clients
**Winner: Mailchimp.** Multi-account dashboard, agency partner program, white-label options on Pro tier. Easier to manage multiple client accounts from one login. ActiveCampaign requires separate accounts per client on most tiers.

### Creator / course seller
**Winner: Neither.** Both are wrong for this use case. Use [Kit](/reviews/kit) (creator-first UX + built-in commerce) or [MailerLite](/reviews/mailerlite) (cheaper + simpler for newsletter-only creators).

### B2B service business (consultants, agencies, coaches)
**Winner: ActiveCampaign.** Built-in CRM + advanced automations + lead scoring. The full package for service businesses that close deals over email.

### Nonprofit / membership organization
**Winner: Mailchimp.** Mailchimp's nonprofit discount (15% off for 501(c)(3) status) and broader feature set fit nonprofits better.

### Small business under 500 contacts, no CRM needs
**Winner: Mailchimp.** Free tier covers 250 contacts. ActiveCampaign's cheapest plan is $29/mo.

### Small business 1,000-5,000 contacts, needs automation
**Winner: ActiveCampaign.** $49/mo Plus or $89/mo Professional gets you automation depth that Mailchimp's $20/mo Essentials or $60/mo Standard can't match.

### Small business 5,000-10,000 contacts, marketing-heavy
**Winner: ActiveCampaign.** $229/mo Professional vs Mailchimp's $350/mo Standard. Saves $121/mo at this tier.

### Small business 10,000+ contacts, any use case
**Winner: ActiveCampaign.** Smooth pricing curve. Mailchimp's pricing becomes punishing at this scale.

## The "should I switch?" question

**Switch from Mailchimp to ActiveCampaign if:**
- You need automation depth Mailchimp can't deliver (conditional branching, CRM-triggered emails)
- You have a built-in CRM need (deal pipelines, lead scoring, sales-team handoffs)
- You're paying Mailchimp's high-tier prices ($350+/mo at 10K contacts) for features you don't use
- Your emails are landing in spam more than expected (ActiveCampaign has better default deliverability)

**Switch from ActiveCampaign to Mailchimp if:**
- You need more than email — ads, social posting, brand kits
- You have a non-technical team that finds ActiveCampaign's automation builder too complex
- You run an ecommerce store and need Mailchimp's Shopify/WooCommerce integration depth
- You want multi-account agency management
- You qualify for Mailchimp's nonprofit discount (15% off)

**Stay where you are if:**
- Your current tool does the job and switching cost (rebuilding automations, retraining team) exceeds savings
- You're under 1,000 contacts and Mailchimp's free tier is working

## Frequently asked questions

### Which is cheaper, Mailchimp or ActiveCampaign?

It depends on list size. Mailchimp is cheaper below 5,000 contacts ($0-100/mo vs ActiveCampaign's $29-129/mo). Above 5,000 contacts, ActiveCampaign is cheaper ($229 vs Mailchimp's $350 at 10K contacts). The crossover point is sharp — Mailchimp's pricing escalates aggressively above 5,000.

### Which has better automation, Mailchimp or ActiveCampaign?

ActiveCampaign has dramatically better automation. The visual builder supports 125+ triggers and actions, conditional splits based on behavior/CRM data/custom fields, and complex multi-branch workflows. Mailchimp's Customer Journeys is comparable to basic ActiveCampaign automations but cannot match the depth.

### Which has better deliverability?

ActiveCampaign has better default deliverability (97-99% inbox placement on shared IP). Mailchimp averages 95-97% on shared IP but matches ActiveCampaign on dedicated IP (Premium tier and above). For most users, the difference is small.

### Which is better for ecommerce?

Mailchimp is better for ecommerce than ActiveCampaign. Mailchimp's Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce integrations are deeper, with product blocks, abandoned cart flows, and revenue attribution built-in. For stores doing $500K+/year, [Klaviyo](/reviews/klaviyo) is better than both.

### Which is better for B2B?

ActiveCampaign is better for B2B. Built-in CRM with deal pipelines, lead scoring, conditional automations triggered on CRM events, and sales-team-friendly reporting. Mailchimp lacks a CRM and would require integration with Salesforce or HubSpot.

### Can I use both Mailchimp and ActiveCampaign?

Technically yes, but it's complex and rarely worth it. Some businesses use Mailchimp for marketing campaigns and ActiveCampaign for sales automations. But you're paying for two tools and maintaining two systems. Pick one based on primary use case.

### Does ActiveCampaign have a free tier?

No. ActiveCampaign's cheapest plan is Lite at $29/mo for 500 contacts. Mailchimp has a free tier that covers 250 contacts. If free matters, Mailchimp wins. If you can pay $29/mo, ActiveCampaign delivers more value.
