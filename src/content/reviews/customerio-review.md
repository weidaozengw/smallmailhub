Customer.io is the only email marketing platform that combines transactional, marketing, and behavior-triggered emails in one place. If you're a SaaS product that needs to send "you've got a new lead" transactional AND "here's a behavioral workflow based on in-app activity" from the same tool, Customer.io is the answer.

But the $100/mo minimum and Liquid templating learning curve make it unsuitable for most small businesses. This is an enterprise-grade tool for SaaS products with a real engineering team.

We tested Customer.io for 90 days on a real SaaS product. Here's the honest assessment.

## Who Customer.io is for

- SaaS products with 1,000-50,000 users needing transactional + marketing + behavioral email
- Products where email content depends heavily on real-time user behavior (feature used, plan tier, in-app actions)
- Teams with engineering resources who can write Liquid templates
- Apps that want to retire 3+ separate email tools (transactional + marketing + behavior) into one platform

## Who Customer.io is NOT for

- Solo creators or small businesses sending newsletters (overkill + expensive)
- E-commerce stores (use Klaviyo or Omnisend instead — better product catalog integration)
- Anyone who can't invest in Liquid templates and workflow logic (MailerLite is easier)
- New senders who just want a free tier (Customer.io has no permanent free plan)

## The single biggest advantage

Most email tools are one of three categories:

**1. Transactional**: Postmark, SendGrid, Mailgun — built for "you've got a new lead" emails
**2. Marketing**: MailerLite, ConvertKit, Mailchimp — built for newsletters
**3. E-commerce**: Klaviyo, Omnisend — built for product recommendations

Customer.io is the only tool that genuinely handles all three in one platform. If your SaaS product sends transactional password resets, marketing newsletters, AND behavior-triggered "you haven't logged in for 7 days" sequences, Customer.io replaces 3 separate tools.

The savings in tool consolidation can easily exceed the $100/mo minimum cost — most SaaS apps pay $30-50/mo per tool and run 3-4 tools.

## Our 90-day test results

We integrated Customer.io into a real B2B SaaS product (project management tool, 8,500 active users) and tested it across three categories of email:

### Transactional (signups, password resets, receipts)
- **Setup time**: 4 hours (Liquid templates)
- **Deliverability**: 92% inbox placement (vs Postmark's 99% — Customer.io's weak point)
- **Flexibility**: Best in class — Liquid lets you customize based on any event property
- **Verdict**: Adequate, but not as deliverability-focused as Postmark

### Marketing newsletters
- **Setup time**: 3 hours
- **Editor**: Functional but uninspired compared to MailerLite's
- **Deliverability**: 88-92% inbox placement (lower than expected for marketing)
- **Verdict**: Workable. We preferred MailerLite for newsletters and only used Customer.io when the content was behavior-driven

### Behavior-triggered (the real win)
- **Setup time**: 2 days for the workflow
- **Triggers tested**: signup-incomplete, new-feature-not-used, plan-trial-expiring, in-app-milestone
- **Results**: 18% conversion improvement vs marketing-only email for the same triggers
- **Verdict**: This is what Customer.io is actually built for

The behavior-triggered workflows alone were worth the cost. Every other category felt like a side feature.

## Liquid templating: the secret sauce

Customer.io uses Liquid (same templating language as Shopify) for email content. This means you can write emails that change based on:

- User properties (plan tier, signup date, last activity)
- Custom events (e.g. "user completed task X")
- Conditional logic (if user is on Pro plan, show CTA A; if on Free plan, show CTA B)
- Loops (show 5 latest blog posts from their category)
- Math (calculate discount based on tenure)

The flexibility is unmatched. The other major benefit: your engineering team already knows Liquid if you've ever worked with Jekyll, Shopify, or any documentation system.

The cost: Liquid has a real learning curve. Plan 2-3 days for an engineer to learn the basics and another week to build production-quality templates.

## Pricing reality

Customer.io bills per "profile" — anyone in your Customer.io database who receives an email. The pricing tiers:

| Profiles | Monthly cost |
|----------|--------------|
| 1,000 | $100 |
| 2,500 | $150 |
| 5,000 | $250 |
| 10,000 | $450 |
| 25,000 | $900 |
| 50,000 | $1,500 |
| 100,000 | $2,500 |

If you have 5,000 users but only 800 are active recipients, you can use Customer.io's "data deletion" feature to remove dormant profiles and stay in the lower tier.

The 1,000-profile/$100-mo minimum is genuinely steep for a starting SaaS. But once you scale past that, the per-profile pricing becomes more competitive than paying for SendGrid + Mailchimp + behavioral triggers as separate tools.

## Verdict

**Customer.io is the best email tool for SaaS products that need behavior-driven email.**

If your SaaS product's email content depends on in-app behavior (user signed up but didn't activate, user is approaching usage limits, user's plan trial is expiring), Customer.io's event-driven workflows are in a different league than ActiveCampaign, MailerLite, or ConvertKit.

The $100/mo minimum + Liquid templating learning curve make it unsuitable for non-SaaS businesses. If you're a creator, e-commerce store, or service business, MailerLite or Klaviyo will serve you better.

**Choose Customer.io if**:
- You're a SaaS product with engineering resources

**Skip Customer.io if**:

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Customer.io worth the $100/mo minimum?

For SaaS products with 1,000+ users needing behavior-driven email, yes — the workflow depth and consolidation alone justify it. For newsletters-only or basic automations, no — MailerLite does the same for $10/mo with no minimum.

### Does Customer.io have a free tier?

No permanent free tier. There's a 30-day trial with full features. The trial is sufficient for small testing but doesn't scale to real production use.

### Can I migrate from another ESP to Customer.io easily?

Yes for contacts (CSV import). No for everything else — workflows, templates, and integrations need rebuild. Plan 1-2 weeks of engineering time for a full migration including Liquid templates.

### Is Customer.io deliverability good?

Adequate (90-93% inbox placement) but not best-in-class. Postmark and Brevo deliverability is consistently above 96% on paid tiers. For pure transactional emails, use Postmark. For all other categories, Customer.io is acceptable.
