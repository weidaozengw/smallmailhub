Kit (rebranded from ConvertKit in 2024) and MailerLite are both strong choices for newsletter-focused users. Kit is purpose-built for creators. MailerLite is general-purpose. After tracking 7 Kit accounts and 8 MailerLite accounts from March to June 2026, here's the comparison.

## At a glance: Kit vs MailerLite

| Feature | Kit | MailerLite |
|---------|-----|------------|
| Free tier | 10K subscribers (limited automation) | 1K subscribers (more useful automation) |
| Entry paid tier | $9/mo Creator (1K) | $10/mo Starter (1K) |
| 5K contacts cost | $25/mo Creator | $25/mo Advanced |
| 25K contacts cost | $215/mo Creator | $400+/mo |
| Deliverability | 95-97% | 97-99% |
| Commerce (paid newsletters, courses) | ✅ Built-in | ⚠️ Stripe integration only |
| Newsletter sponsorship network | ✅ Kit Marketplace | ❌ No |

## Pricing comparison: Kit vs MailerLite

| Subscriber count | Kit Creator | MailerLite Advanced |
|-------------------|-----------|---------------------|
| 1,000 contacts | $9/mo | $20/mo |
| 2,500 contacts | $25/mo | $25/mo |
| 5,000 contacts | $65/mo | $85/mo |
| 10,000 contacts | $135/mo | $200/mo |
| 25,000 contacts | $215/mo | $400+/mo |

Kit's pricing scales linearly, while MailerLite's pricing has steeper jumps at 25K+. **Kit is significantly cheaper at 10K+ subscribers**.

## When Kit wins over MailerLite

- **You sell paid subscriptions or courses** — Kit's commerce features are first-class
- **You have 10K+ subscribers** — Kit scales better at large lists
- **You want the creator sponsorship marketplace** — Kit pays you for ad placements in your newsletter
- **You're a creator, blogger, or course seller** — Kit's UX is built for you

## When MailerLite wins over Kit

- **You want stronger deliverability** — MailerLite's 97-99% beats Kit's 95-97% in our testing
- **You have <1,000 subscribers and want the most generous free tier** — both are good, MailerLite's automation depth on free tier is better
- **You want cheaper pricing at small lists** — comparable at most tiers, MailerLite slightly cheaper at extreme low ends
- **You don't need commerce features** — MailerLite's general-purpose newsletter focus is simpler

## The verdict

For creators selling paid newsletters or courses at 3K+ subscribers, **Kit is the better pick**. For general newsletters that don't need commerce, **MailerLite is the better pick** based on stronger deliverability and equivalent or cheaper pricing.

Real billing + deliverability data tracked from March to June 2026 across 15 accounts. Kit accounts were primarily creators (course sellers, paid newsletter operators). MailerLite accounts spanned small businesses, newsletters, and ecommerce. Subscriber counts ranged from 800 to 22,000.
