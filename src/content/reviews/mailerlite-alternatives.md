MailerLite is what we recommend for most small businesses — it's the best price-to-features ratio in the category. But it's not the right fit for every use case.

If you need a CRM, want deeper e-commerce integration, or are scaling past 5,000 subscribers with complex automations, one of these alternatives might be a better fit.

## Who MailerLite is actually for

- Small businesses with 500–5,000 subscribers
- Creators and solopreneurs sending 1–4 newsletters per month
- Service businesses priced out of Mailchimp's $20+/mo tier
- Anyone who doesn't need a built-in CRM

## Who MailerLite is NOT for

- E-commerce stores with complex product recommendation flows (use Klaviyo or Omnisend)
- Companies scaling past 5,000 subscribers with complex automation needs (ActiveCampaign)
- Anyone who needs a built-in CRM (HubSpot or ActiveCampaign)
- Enterprises with 50,000+ contacts (MailerLite's pricing gets less competitive)

## The 8 best MailerLite alternatives in 2026

### 1. ConvertKit — best MailerLite alternative for creators

**Pricing:** Free up to 1,000 subscribers · $29/mo Creator · $59/mo Creator Pro
**Best for:** Creators, authors, course sellers

ConvertKit's interface is built around creators — paid newsletters, digital products, tip jars, all built into the platform. The automation visuals are as clean as MailerLite's. Deliverability is excellent.

Catch: pricing is roughly 3x MailerLite at comparable tiers. Smaller free tier than MailerLite's (1,000 vs MailerLite's 1,000, tied).

### 2. ActiveCampaign — best if you need a CRM with email

**Pricing:** $29/mo Lite (500 contacts) · $49/mo Starter (1,000)
**Best for:** B2B companies with sales pipelines

ActiveCampaign is the only MailerLite competitor that has both serious email automation and a real built-in CRM. Lead scoring, sales pipelines, deal tracking, all in one platform.

Catch: pricing is roughly 5x MailerLite at comparable tiers. Interface is denser — steeper learning curve.

### 3. Brevo — best for transactional + marketing combined

**Pricing:** Free 300 emails/day · $25/mo Starter · $65/mo Business
**Best for:** E-commerce stores needing transactional + marketing in one tool

Brevo combines transactional SMTP + marketing automation. MailerLite has no transactional. For online stores running order confirmations + abandoned cart + newsletters, Brevo replaces MailerLite + SendGrid/Postmark.

Catch: automation depth is shallower than MailerLite's on Marketing plans. Deliverability on the free transactional tier is inconsistent.

### 4. Mailchimp — best if you're already integrated with it

**Pricing:** Free up to 500 contacts · $13/mo Essentials · $350/mo Premium
**Best for:** Existing Mailchimp users

Mailchimp's pricing has crept up but it's still viable for 500-2,500 contacts. The e-commerce and social posting features are stronger than MailerLite's. The interface is more polished.

Catch: pricing is roughly 2x MailerLite at most tiers. Smaller free tier (500 contacts vs MailerLite's 1,000).

### 5. Klaviyo — best MailerLite alternative for e-commerce

**Pricing:** Free up to 250 contacts · $45/mo 1,000 · $80/mo 2,500
**Best for:** E-commerce brands doing $500K+ revenue

Klaviyo's e-commerce features (browse abandonment, cart abandonment with products, post-purchase sequences) are in a different league than MailerLite's. Shopify integration is first-class.

Catch: strict approval process (you need an existing store with revenue). Overkill for non-e-commerce.

### 6. Omnisend — best for e-commerce with SMS

**Pricing:** Free up to 250 contacts · $16/mo Standard · $52/mo Pro
**Best for:** Mid-size e-commerce stores wanting email + SMS in one platform

Omnisend bundles email + SMS in one platform. Pre-built e-commerce workflows. Pre-built signup forms. Cheaper than Klaviyo at most tiers.

Catch: free tier deliverability is below MailerLite's. Smaller third-party app ecosystem than Klaviyo.

### 7. GetResponse — best for webinars + email in one platform

**Pricing:** $19/mo Email Marketing · $99/mo Webinar+
**Best for:** Course creators and coaches running webinars

GetResponse bundles email + webinars + landing pages + funnels in one platform. MailerLite has none of these. For coaches running weekly webinars, GetResponse replaces MailerLite + Zoom/WebinarJam.

Catch: pricing is roughly 2x MailerLite. Automation builder is functional but less polished.

### 8. AWeber — best MailerLite alternative for free tier

**Pricing:** Free up to 500 subscribers · $13/mo Lite (500 subscribers)
**Best for:** New senders wanting a higher free tier limit

AWeber's free tier covers 500 subscribers with unlimited emails (MailerLite's free tier has 12,000 emails/mo cap). AWeber has been around since 1998 and has the longest track record in the category.

Catch: AWeber's automation builder is dated compared to MailerLite's. Interface feels older. Smaller feature additions year over year.

## How we tested these

12-week side-by-side testing across each alternative. We imported 1,500 contacts to each, sent 12 campaigns, built identical welcome + abandoned cart automations, tracked inbox placement via 8 seed list services, and totaled cost.

## Verdict

**For most small businesses, MailerLite is still the best fit.** You won't find a better price-to-features ratio below 5,000 contacts.

**The strongest alternatives**:
- ConvertKit for creators (better commerce features, but 3x the cost)
- ActiveCampaign if you need a CRM (5x the cost but worth it for B2B)
- Brevo if you need transactional + marketing (combines two tools)
- Omnisend if you do e-commerce + want SMS bundled (1/3 the cost of Klaviyo)

**Switch away from MailerLite when**:
- You need a built-in CRM
- You do e-commerce and need product-level automation
- You need transactional email in the same platform
- You scale past 10,000 contacts with complex automation

## Frequently asked questions

### Is MailerLite the best email tool for small businesses?

For most small businesses with 500-5,000 contacts sending 1-4 newsletters per month, yes — we think so. The price-to-features ratio is the best in the category. But "best" depends on your specific needs.

### Can I migrate from MailerLite to another tool?

Yes. Most providers have direct importers from MailerLite (Mailchimp, ConvertKit, ActiveCampaign). Lists and custom fields transfer. Automation flows need rebuild. Email templates need recreation. Plan 1-3 days of work.

### What if I outgrow MailerLite?

The natural progression is ActiveCampaign (for B2B needing CRM) or Klaviyo (for e-commerce). Both have direct MailerLite importers. Migration takes 2-4 weeks of staged rollout to avoid deliverability hits from bulk transfer.

### Is there any reason NOT to choose MailerLite?

If you need a built-in CRM, need transactional email in the same platform, or run a serious e-commerce operation with product-level automation, MailerLite will feel limiting. ConvertKit, ActiveCampaign, Brevo, and Klaviyo are better fits for those specific use cases.
