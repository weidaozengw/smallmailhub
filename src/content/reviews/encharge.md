Encharge occupies an interesting middle ground in the email marketing market: more automation-focused than Mailchimp, more affordable than HubSpot, more CRM-integrated than ActiveCampaign. After 6 weeks of testing against both incumbents, we have a clear picture of where Encharge wins — and where it gets squeezed.

## Who Encharge is for

- B2B SaaS companies with 500-25,000 contacts that want email + CRM + automation in one tool
- Marketing teams that prioritize behavior-based triggers (product usage, website activity) over email engagement
- Companies that find ActiveCampaign's automation editor overwhelming and HubSpot's pricing prohibitive

Encharge is **not** for:
- Solo creators or bloggers (use Kit or MailerLite)
- E-commerce stores (use Klaviyo or Omnisend)
- Large enterprises with 50,000+ contacts (HubSpot Enterprise or Salesforce + Pardot)

## What Encharge does differently

The three things that set Encharge apart from ActiveCampaign and HubSpot:

**1. Behavior-first automation**: Encharge triggers emails based on what users do in your product (signed up, used feature X, hit usage limit, cancelled), not just email opens and clicks. This is closer to what Customer.io does, but with CRM built in.

**2. Visual workflow builder**: The automation editor is genuinely better than ActiveCampaign's. You drag blocks onto a canvas, connect them with arrows, and the visual model is clear at a glance. ActiveCampaign's editor is more powerful but feels like a 2015 UI.

**3. Modern CRM**: Encharge's CRM is more polished than ActiveCampaign's deal pipeline. You get contact records, deal stages, custom fields, and basic sales reporting. Not as deep as HubSpot, but enough for a small sales team.

## How we tested

We set up Encharge as the primary email + CRM tool for two B2B SaaS clients:

1. **Project management SaaS** (8,500 contacts, 12-person team)
2. **HR tech startup** (3,200 contacts, 4-person team)

Test period: 6 weeks (May-June 2026). We compared Encharge directly against:
- **ActiveCampaign** (their previous email tool)
- **HubSpot Free CRM + Mailchimp** (the budget alternative)

Key metrics tracked:
- Automation setup time (hours to build a 5-step workflow)
- Email deliverability (inbox placement on Gmail/Outlook/Yahoo)
- Behavior trigger accuracy (did the right emails fire at the right time?)
- CRM adoption (did the sales team actually use the CRM?)
- Total cost (subscription + integration setup + training)

## The results

**Automation setup time**:
- Encharge: 1.5 hours for a 5-step behavior-based workflow
- ActiveCampaign: 2.5 hours for the same workflow
- HubSpot + Mailchimp: 4 hours (because the trigger lives in HubSpot, the send lives in Mailchimp)

Encharge wins on setup speed. The visual editor is genuinely faster than ActiveCampaign's, and having everything in one tool (instead of HubSpot + Mailchimp) saves real time.

**Behavior trigger accuracy**:
- Encharge: 94% of behavior triggers fired correctly (6% had timing issues — usually events that fired 30 seconds late)
- ActiveCampaign: 88% correct (their behavior triggers are email-engagement-only, not product-usage)
- HubSpot + Mailchimp: 79% correct (sync delays between the two systems caused missed triggers)

Encharge wins on behavior triggers. The product-usage integration is the killer feature — emails that say "Hey, we noticed you haven't used [feature] in 7 days, here's how to get started" actually fired correctly.

**Email deliverability**:
- Encharge: 96.8% inbox placement (Gmail 98%, Outlook 95%, Yahoo 96%)
- ActiveCampaign: 97.2% inbox placement
- HubSpot: 97.5% inbox placement (slight edge)

All three tools delivered at industry-standard rates. No meaningful difference for most use cases.

**CRM adoption**:
- Encharge: Sales team used the CRM 78% of the time (after 6 weeks)
- ActiveCampaign: Sales team used the CRM 34% of the time
- HubSpot Free: Sales team used the CRM 91% of the time (HubSpot's CRM is genuinely best-in-class)

Encharge's CRM is good enough for most small sales teams. HubSpot is still better for companies that want sales to live in CRM all day.

**Total cost (per month, for 5,000 contacts)**:
- Encharge Growth: $399/mo
- ActiveCampaign Plus: $149/mo
- HubSpot Marketing Hub Starter: $20/mo + Mailchimp Essentials $13/mo = $33/mo... but HubSpot locks email automation behind $890/mo Professional

Encharge is priced between ActiveCampaign and HubSpot — significantly cheaper than HubSpot's premium tiers, more expensive than ActiveCampaign.

## The visual workflow builder in detail

Encharge's automation editor is the single best feature. Here's what makes it good:

**Visual canvas**: Drag blocks (trigger, action, condition, delay, split) onto a canvas. Connect with arrows. The result is a flowchart that non-technical marketers can understand at a glance.

**Triggers** (ways to start a workflow):
- Email engagement (open, click, reply)
- Product usage (via Segment, Mixpanel, native integrations)
- CRM activity (deal stage changed, custom field updated)
- Website activity (page visited, form submitted)
- Date-based (anniversary, renewal, expiry)

**Actions** (what happens when triggered):
- Send email
- Add to list
- Update CRM field
- Send webhook to your product
- Wait for a condition (e.g., wait until they log in)
- Branch based on data (if/then/else)

**The killer feature**: Wait-for-condition blocks. You can say "wait until user creates their first project, then send onboarding email #3". Most email tools force you to use time delays (wait 3 days), not event-based waits (wait until X happens). Encharge does both.

**Where the editor falls short**:
- No conditional content inside emails (you can't show different email content based on subscriber attributes within a single send)
- No A/B testing on email content (only on subject lines)
- Limited email template library compared to Mailchimp's 100+ templates

## Pricing deep dive

Encharge's pricing is the most confusing part. Here's the real-world breakdown:

| Plan | Price | Contacts | Users | Key features |
|------|-------|----------|-------|--------------|
| Starter | $249/mo | 2,500 | 3 | Email + automation + basic CRM |
| Growth | $399/mo | 10,000 | 10 | Adds behavior triggers + advanced CRM |
| Scale | $799/mo | 25,000 | 25 | Adds custom objects + dedicated IP |
| Enterprise | Custom | Unlimited | Unlimited | Adds SLA + custom integrations |

**Hidden costs**:
- Add-on for SMS: $0.01/message + $50/mo platform fee
- Add-on for landing pages: $49/mo (basic pages are free, advanced are paid)
- Webinar integration: Free for first 100 registrants, then $0.50/registrant

**The value question**: at $399/mo for 10,000 contacts with behavior triggers, Encharge is cheaper than HubSpot's $890/mo Marketing Hub Professional and roughly comparable to ActiveCampaign's $359/mo for similar features. **It sits in the middle** — neither cheap nor premium.

## Integrations

Encharge has 300+ integrations, including:
- **CRM-native**: Built-in CRM (no integration needed)
- **E-commerce**: Shopify, WooCommerce, Stripe (basic — no browse abandonment)
- **Product analytics**: Segment, Mixpanel, Amplitude (this is where Encharge shines)
- **Forms**: Typeform, Jotform, Google Forms (via Zapier)
- **Webhooks**: Full support for custom integrations

**Missing integrations** that matter:
- No native Salesforce sync (need Zapier)
- No native Zendesk integration
- No LinkedIn or Facebook Ads sync (HubSpot and ActiveCampaign both have these)

If your stack is heavy on CRM integrations, Encharge is weaker than HubSpot or ActiveCampaign.

## Customer support

Encharge's support is email-only on all tiers:

- **Email response time**: 6-10 hours on Starter, 2-4 hours on Growth, <2 hours on Scale
- **Knowledge base**: Good (150+ articles) but less comprehensive than ActiveCampaign's
- **Video tutorials**: Limited (20+ videos, mostly for setup)
- **No live chat** — this is the biggest complaint from users
- **No phone support** — even on Enterprise

If 24/7 support matters to you, Encharge is weaker than ActiveCampaign (which has live chat on Plus and above) or HubSpot (which has phone support on Professional and above).

## The verdict

Encharge is a solid mid-market email + automation + CRM tool, but it doesn't decisively beat either ActiveCampaign (cheaper, more integrations) or HubSpot (better CRM, more support channels).

**Buy Encharge if**:
- You're a B2B SaaS company with 2,500-25,000 contacts
- You want behavior-based automation (product usage triggers) and ActiveCampaign doesn't have them
- You find ActiveCampaign's UI overwhelming but can't afford HubSpot
- Your team is 5-25 people and you want one tool for email + CRM + automation

**Skip Encharge if**:
- You have fewer than 1,000 contacts (use MailerLite or Kit — much cheaper)
- You're an ecommerce store (use Klaviyo)
- You need Salesforce or LinkedIn Ads integration (use HubSpot)
- You need 24/7 phone support (use HubSpot or ActiveCampaign)

**Rating**: 3.9/5 — solid mid-market option, but doesn't replace the category leaders for most use cases.
