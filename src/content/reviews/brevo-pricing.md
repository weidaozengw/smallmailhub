Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) rebranded in March 2023 and restructured pricing again in November 2024. The result is a tier structure that's reasonable at the small end and aggressively enterprise at the top — most small businesses end up on Free, Lite, or Starter. This breakdown covers actual Brevo pricing across all three pricing categories: Marketing Platform, Transactional, and Sales, with real costs at every send volume tier.

## At a glance: 2026 Brevo pricing

| Plan | Marketing Platform | Transactional | Sales | Best for |
|------|-------------------|---------------|-------|----------|
| **Free** | 300 emails/day (9,000/mo) | 20,000/mo (no separate signup) | Not included | Hobbyists, very low-volume senders |
| **Lite** | $9/mo (20,000 emails/mo) | Same as Free (~20K/mo) | Not included | Solo creators, micro businesses |
| **Starter** | $18/mo (20,000 emails/mo) | Same | Included | Small businesses up to 50K emails/mo |
| **Standard** | $449/mo (150,000 emails/mo) | Same | Included | Mid-market, multi-brand senders |
| **Premium** | $989/mo (1,000,000 emails/mo) | Same | Included | Enterprise, transactional-heavy |

**Two things to understand upfront**:

1. Brevo's pricing is **send-based, not contact-based** — you pay for the number of emails sent, not for the number of contacts in your list. Send 5,000 emails to 50,000 contacts? Brevo counts the 5,000 sends, not your 50,000 contact list.
2. Marketing Platform and Transactional are **separate products** in Brevo's billing. You can subscribe to just Marketing Platform and skip Transactional entirely if you don't send order confirmations or password resets through Brevo.

This send-based model is **fundamentally different from Mailchimp and ConvertKit** (which charge per contact). For businesses with large lists but infrequent sends — like monthly newsletters to a 30,000-subscriber base — Brevo can be dramatically cheaper.

## The 2026 tier walk-through

### Free — what you actually get

The Brevo Free tier is **generous for a no-cost plan**, but with critical limits:

- ✅ Up to 300 emails/day (roughly 9,000/month)
- ✅ Unlimited contacts
- ✅ Email + SMS + WhatsApp campaigns
- ✅ Signup forms, list management, basic segmentation
- ✅ Transactional email: 20,000 emails/month
- ❌ **No marketing automation** (multi-step sequences require Lite+)
- ❌ **No A/B testing**
- ❌ **No advanced segmentation**
- ❌ Brevo logo on transactional emails (removable on Lite+)
- ❌ Daily send cap — can't stockpile

The Free tier's biggest trap: **the 300 emails/day limit**. A single campaign blast to 3,500 subscribers takes ~12 days. If you send a weekly newsletter to even 500 subscribers, you'll consistently hit the daily cap.

**Where Free works**: Side projects, very low-volume senders (under 250 emails/day), transactional-only setups with a separate marketing ESP.

### Lite ($9/mo annual / $11/mo monthly) — the most common starting point

This is the **real entry-level plan** for most small businesses.

- ✅ 20,000 emails/month (no daily cap)
- ✅ Marketing automation
- ✅ A/B testing
- ✅ Advanced segmentation
- ✅ 3 users
- ✅ No Brevo logo

At 20,000 emails per month, you can send a weekly newsletter to 5,000 subscribers (4 × 5,000 = 20,000) without overage fees. That covers the majority of small businesses with under 5,000 subscribers.

**The Lite → Starter jump is the cheapest real upgrade** — from $9 to $18, you get:
- 4 users (vs 3)
- Advanced reporting
- Multi-channel campaigns (email + SMS + WhatsApp combined workflows)
- Sales pipeline (basic CRM)
- Priority support

Most businesses never need to go above Starter.

### Standard ($449/mo) and Premium ($989/mo) — enterprise territory

These tiers are designed for multi-brand senders, agencies, and businesses with multi-million annual email volume. Pricing for Standard and Premium includes:

- Larger send volumes (150K and 1M emails/mo respectively)
- Multi-account management
- Dedicated IP option
- Advanced deliverability consultation
- Phone support (Premium only)
- SAML SSO (Premium only)

For small businesses, these tiers are essentially out of scope. **We don't recommend them unless you have a specific need** (e.g., dedicated IP for high-volume transactional email, or sub-account management for an agency with multiple clients).

## Transactional email pricing — Brevo's standout feature

This is where Brevo genuinely differs from competitors. Unlike Mailchimp (which requires the separate Mandrill service for transactional) or MailerLite (which doesn't include transactional at all), Brevo bundles transactional email with the main product:

- **Free tier**: 20,000 transactional emails/month included
- **Lite and above**: transactional sends consume from your overall send budget; overages at ~$0.001/email
- **No separate signup** for transactional
- **All API features** (SMTP relay, webhooks, templates) available across all tiers

For a typical e-commerce store sending 10,000 transactional emails per month (order confirmations, shipping notifications, password resets) plus 10,000 marketing emails per month, **Brevo's 20,000 emails/month on Lite is essentially free for both marketing and transactional**. The next jump (Standard at 150,000 emails/mo) costs $449/mo but handles most stores doing <$10M/year revenue.

By comparison:
- **Mailchimp + Mandrill**: $13/mo (Mailchimp Essentials) + $20/mo (Mandrill base) + ~$0.0010/email = $33/mo minimum, plus per-email costs
- **Brevo Lite**: $9/mo (everything bundled)

**For transactional-heavy businesses, Brevo saves $25+/month on the equivalent Mailchimp setup.**

## What changed in 2026

The most material pricing changes since Brevo's November 2024 restructure:

- **Free tier daily limit increased** from 200 to 300 emails/day (more generous)
- **Marketing automation** removed from Free tier (previously included)
- **Starter tier** added multi-channel campaigns (email + SMS + WhatsApp combined workflows)
- **Sales pipeline** included in Starter (previously Standard-only)
- **Annual prepay discount** increased from 15% to 20%

If you signed up before November 2024, you may still have free marketing automation on the Free tier — this was a one-time grandfathered feature in the original Sendinblue pricing. Confirm with support if you're not sure.

## The verdict

Brevo pricing in 2026 is **competitive across most small business tiers** and **aggressively cheaper than Mailchimp for businesses with large lists and low send volume**.

**If you send over 50K emails/month to a list of 10K+ contacts**, Brevo's send-based pricing saves you $40-50/month vs Mailchimp Essentials or Standard tiers.

**If you send transactional email** (order confirmations, signups, etc.), Brevo saves you $25+/month vs Mailchimp + Mandrill.

**If you only have 200-500 subscribers and send weekly**, both Mailchimp and MailerLite offer similar value at the entry tier — pick by feature, not price.

**If you're sending less than 300 emails/day**, the Brevo Free tier genuinely works for hobby projects, but you'll hit the daily cap quickly if you try to run a real business on it.
