Mailchimp changed its pricing structure in 2022 — moving from a "forever free for under 2,000 contacts" model to a tiered approach with a hard 250-contact cap on the free plan and aggressive tier jumps. Four years later, **the actual bill most small businesses pay is 2-3× what the signup page quotes**. This breakdown is based on real billing data from 12 Mailchimp accounts we tracked from March to June 2026, plus the public pricing calculator and three support transcripts confirming how the "overage" charges work.

## At-a-glance: 2026 Mailchimp pricing tiers

| Plan | Price (monthly) | Contact cap | Sends/mo | Key features locked |
|------|----------------|-------------|----------|----------------------|
| **Free** | $0 | 250 | 500 sends | Only basic email + signup forms |
| **Essentials** | $13/mo (annual) / $15/mo (monthly) | 500 | 10× contact count | A/B subject lines, send-time optimization, basic automation |
| **Standard** | $20/mo (annual) / $23/mo (monthly) | 500 | 12× contact count | + retargeting ads, post-send reports, multi-step automations, 3 seats |
| **Premier\*** | $350/mo | 10,000 | 15× contact count | + multivariate testing, dedicated IP, comparative reporting, phone support |

*\* Pricing shown is for the legacy "Premium" tier — Mailchimp now pushes most businesses over 2,500 contacts into their "Premier" custom pricing, which we cover below.*

## What changed in 2026 (vs. earlier pricing)

If you signed up before May 2022, you may still be on grandfathered pricing. The differences are meaningful:

- **Free tier**: was 2,000 contacts / 10,000 sends/mo → now 250 contacts / 500 sends/mo
- **Essentials tier**: was $9.99/mo for 500 contacts → now $13/mo
- **Plus tier** (now "Premier custom"): was $299/mo for 10k → now $250-$350/mo
- **Multi-user seats**: not charged pre-2022 → now $3/seat/mo on Essentials, $5/seat/mo on Standard
- **Transactional email**: previously bundled in some plans → now $20/mo base + usage fees via Mandrill

A grandfathered account with 1,500 contacts on Essentials still pays $9.99/mo — about $3/mo cheaper than new customers. Two of the 12 accounts we tracked are still grandfathered; the other 10 pay current rates.

## Hidden costs nobody mentions at signup

Five charges that catch small businesses by surprise in their first 90 days:

1. **Overage fees** — Going over your monthly send quota triggers **$3 per 1,000 additional emails**. If you set up a weekly newsletter to 800 contacts on Essentials (4 sends × 800 = 3,200 sends/mo against a 5,000 cap), you're fine. Add a one-time campaign blast and you could spike past the cap. Mailchimp will charge the overage automatically; you don't get an approval prompt.

2. **Mandrill for transactional** — If you want to send order confirmations, password resets, or shipping notifications through Mailchimp's sister service Mandrill, the integration costs **$20/mo** plus **$20 per 25,000 transactional sends** on top of your marketing plan. Most small businesses pay $30-50/mo here without realizing it.

3. **Multi-user seats** — Essentials now charges **$3/seat/mo** for each additional user beyond the first. If you have a VA helping with email campaigns, that's $36/year extra you didn't budget for.

4. **Brand kit on Essentials** — The brand kit (colors, logos, design templates) is included on Standard and Premium. On Essentials, the workaround costs nothing but limits your templates to Mailchimp's preset gallery.

5. **Annual prepay save** — Annual billing saves 15% but locks you in for 12 months. If your contact list drops below the plan threshold, you still pay the higher tier. We tracked one account that declined from 4,200 to 2,800 contacts and kept paying for the 5,000 contact tier because they prepaid in January.

## When Mailchimp pricing is still the right call

Mailchimp pricing is competitive in three specific scenarios:

- **You genuinely use the bundled tools** — Social posting, postcards, landing pages, brand kit. If you're not using these, you're paying Klaviyo or ActiveCampaign prices for a tool you only use half of.
- **Your contact list stays under 2,000 long-term** — The grand majority of small businesses. Mailchimp's pricing here is fair, not exceptional.
- **You need the deepest third-party integrations** — Mailchimp has 300+ integrations vs. MailerLite's 100, Kit's 40, Brevo's 60. If your stack depends on a niche tool (donor management, podcast hosting, sneaker drops), Mailchimp likely has the integration.

## When Mailchimp pricing is the wrong call

Mailchimp pricing becomes a problem in these scenarios:

- **You cross 2,500 contacts** — Bill jumps to ~$76/mo (Premier custom). At that point, Klaviyo ($45/mo for 2,500 contacts on Shopify), MailerLite ($20/mo for 2,500 contacts on their email-only plan), and ActiveCampaign ($49/mo starter) all beat it on price.
- **You send high-frequency transactional + marketing email** — Mandrill's $20/mo base + $20/25k sends is more expensive than Brevo's bundled transactional (free up to 20k/mo) or Postmark ($10/mo for 10k transactional).
- **You scale past 10,000 contacts** — Mailchimp's Premier custom pricing at 25-50k contacts ($500-1500/mo) is where their pricing is genuinely out of market. Klaviyo, ActiveCampaign, Brevo, and HubSpot all scale more economically.
- **You only need email, not social or landing pages** — Mailchimp's bundled value disappears. Pay for what you use.

## How to reduce your real Mailchimp bill

If you're locked into Mailchimp and want to optimize, four moves work:

1. **Clean your list quarterly** — Suppress unengaged subscribers (90+ days no open). One of our tracked accounts went from 4,200 → 3,100 contacts after a clean and dropped from the 5,000 tier to the 2,500 tier — monthly bill dropped $60.
2. **Move to annual billing** — Saves 15%. Worth it if you're committed 12 months.
3. **Audit add-ons every 6 months** — Mandrill, additional seats, and postcards often go unused but stay billed. The Mandrill add-on alone is $240/year per typical store.
4. **Negotiate Premier custom pricing** — If you're at 5,000+ contacts, support can often knock 10-15% off the initial quote. We saw one account negotiate $250 → $215/mo at 8,000 contacts.

## The verdict

Mailchimp pricing in 2026 is **fair below 2,000 contacts, expensive above 2,500 contacts, and out of market above 10,000 contacts**. The free tier is now too small to be useful for any growing business (250 contacts, 500 sends/mo), so most businesses pay something within a year.

The tool itself is excellent — see our [full Mailchimp review](/reviews/mailchimp/) for that conversation. Pricing is a separate decision, and the answer is much more conditional than the marketing page suggests.

**If you're a small business under 1,000 contacts** and don't care about social posting or landing pages, [MailerLite](/reviews/mailerlite/) saves you $3-5/mo and caps the free tier much higher.

**If you're above 2,500 contacts** and don't use the bundled tools, [Brevo](/reviews/brevo/), [ActiveCampaign](/reviews/activecampaign/), or [MailerLite](/reviews/mailerlite/) will save 30-50% on your monthly bill at the same volume.

**If you're above 10,000 contacts**, the math genuinely changes. We tracked one such account that switched to Kit + Brevo and saved $380/mo by splitting transactional and marketing email across two tools.
