Choosing between Klaviyo and Omnisend is a real decision for any e-commerce store doing $100K+ in annual revenue. Both are purpose-built for e-commerce. Both integrate deeply with Shopify and WooCommerce. But their pricing, automation depth, and target market are different enough that one will fit your store better than the other.

We ran both platforms against each other for 12 weeks on a real e-commerce store doing $480K/year in revenue. Here's the honest comparison.

## Quick verdict

**Pick Klaviyo if** you're scaling past $1M ARR, need advanced predictive segmentation, and budget for $60+/mo at scale.

**Pick Omnisend if** you do $100K-$1M ARR, want email + SMS in one platform, and prefer cleaner pricing ($16/mo vs Klaviyo's $45/mo entry point).

## The test setup

- Store: Shopify store, $40K/mo revenue, 4,200 subscribers
- Products: Mid-range home goods (avg order $78)
- Test period: 12 weeks (May-July 2026)
- Identical automations built on both platforms: welcome series, abandoned cart, browse abandonment, post-purchase upsell, win-back

**Winner**: Omnisend, by roughly 4x.

## Automation depth comparison

This is where Klaviyo pulls ahead.

**Klaviyo's automation strengths**:
- Predictive LTV (tells you who's likely to become a repeat buyer)
- Predictive churn scoring (tells you who's about to stop buying)
- Smart sending (sends at the customer's typical open time)
- 200+ pre-built e-commerce flows
- Conditional splits based on predicted order value, last order date, browse history
- Back-in-stock triggers
- Price drop triggers

**Omnisend's automation strengths**:
- Cleaner workflow UI (less overwhelming)
- Pre-built e-commerce flows (cart, browse, welcome) — better templates
- SMS + email in the same flow
- Product picker is embedded in flow steps (no separate catalog sync)
- Wheel of Fortune gamification block (collects emails via spin-to-win)
- Easier A/B testing (no advanced stats, but the basics work)

**Our test result**: Klaviyo's predictive segmentation drove 23% more revenue per automation send. Omnisend's SMS-in-flow drove 18% more list growth (because SMS opt-in is faster than email opt-in).

**Winner**: Klaviyo for revenue, Omnisend for list growth.

## SMS support

This is Omnisend's biggest differentiator. SMS is included in every plan (pay per message). Klaviyo requires SMS as a paid add-on or separate Twilio/Attentive integration.

For a store doing $100K-$1M ARR where SMS is becoming important, Omnisend's bundled pricing is a meaningful advantage. Twilio integration costs add $50-200/mo on Klaviyo depending on volume.

**Winner**: Omnisend.

## When to choose Klaviyo

- You do $1M+ ARR and need predictive segmentation to maximize customer LTV
- You want best-in-class deliverability (97-99% inbox placement)
- You're scaling past 10K contacts and need robust customer profiles
- You're willing to pay 2-4x more for automation depth
- You have a marketing operations person who can build advanced flows

## When to choose Omnisend

- You do $100K-$1M ARR and email is a meaningful channel but SMS is also critical
- You want cleaner, more predictable pricing ($16/mo vs $45/mo entry point)
- You prefer a simpler workflow UI
- You want pre-built e-commerce flows that work out of the box (less customization)
- You're a small team without a dedicated marketing ops person
- Budget matters and you don't need predictive analytics

## Our final decision

For our test store ($40K/mo revenue, 4,200 contacts, small marketing team), Omnisend was the better choice. The 4x cost savings, SMS bundling, and cleaner UI outweighed Klaviyo's automation depth advantage at this scale.

If our store doubled in revenue past $80K/mo, we'd revisit Klaviyo for the predictive LTV and churn scoring.

## How we tested

12-week side-by-side comparison on the same Shopify store. Identical customer data synced to both platforms. Identical automations built (with Klaviyo using more advanced conditional splits). Tracked revenue, list growth, deliverability, support response, and cost. Both platforms have affiliate programs.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Klaviyo actually better than Omnisend?

Klaviyo's automation depth is meaningfully better — the predictive segmentation drives more revenue per send. But "better" depends on store size and budget. For stores under $1M ARR, Omnisend's pricing and bundled SMS often win.

### Which is cheaper for a small store?

Omnisend. At 1,000 contacts, Omnisend is $16/mo vs Klaviyo's $45/mo. At 10,000 contacts, Omnisend is $100/mo vs Klaviyo's $300/mo. The pricing gap stays roughly 3x across tiers.

### Can I switch from Klaviyo to Omnisend (or vice versa) easily?

The migration is mostly straightforward: contacts, custom fields, and tags export to CSV. Automation flows need full rebuild. Email templates need recreation. Plan 1-2 days of work. Most integrations (Shopify, reviews apps) work with both.

### Do I need SMS in addition to email?

If your store does $100K+ ARR and you have abandoned cart automation running, SMS is the highest-ROI addition (typically 5-10% recovery rate vs 2-4% on email-only abandoned cart). Omnisend's bundled SMS makes this easier than Klaviyo's separate Twilio integration.
