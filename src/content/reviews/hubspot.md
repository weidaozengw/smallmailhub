HubSpot is the 800-pound gorilla of marketing software. What started as a side project in 2006 has grown into a platform with 247,000+ customers and $2.17 billion in annual revenue. The pitch is simple: replace your CRM, email tool, landing page builder, form tool, live chat, and sales sequencing tool with one platform. After testing HubSpot as our primary marketing platform for six months across two B2B service businesses and one ecommerce client, we have strong opinions about whether this all-in-one approach actually delivers.

## Who HubSpot is for

- B2B service businesses with sales teams that need CRM + email in one place
- SaaS companies with complex buyer journeys (lead nurturing, scoring, handoff to sales)
- Marketing teams that want to consolidate tools and reduce vendor sprawl
- Companies with budget for $890+/mo tiers (or willingness to start on the free tier and grow)

HubSpot is **not** for solo creators, bloggers, or small ecommerce stores on a budget — MailerLite, ConvertKit, or Mailchimp will serve those audiences better at a fraction of the cost.

## What you get in the free tier

This is where HubSpot shines and where most reviewers get confused. The free tier is genuinely useful, not a crippled demo:

- **Free CRM**: Unlimited contacts, unlimited deals, unlimited users, contact activity timeline, basic reporting
- **Free Marketing Hub**: 2,000 emails per month, drag-and-drop email editor, basic forms, basic landing pages, ad management, conversation inbox
- **Free Sales Hub**: Up to 5 users, email tracking, meeting scheduler, basic sequences
- **Free Service Hub**: Ticket management, knowledge base, conversation routing

The catch: HubSpot branding appears on your emails, landing pages, and forms unless you upgrade to a paid tier. For early-stage startups this is acceptable. For established businesses it's embarrassing.

The other catch: free tier limits are per-feature, not unified. You can have unlimited CRM contacts but only send 2,000 marketing emails per month. Need more emails? Upgrade to Marketing Hub Starter ($20/mo). Need email automation? Upgrade to Marketing Hub Professional ($890/mo). The tiering is the trick.

## How we tested

We set up HubSpot as the primary email + CRM platform for two B2B service businesses (a consultancy and a creative agency, both 5-10 employees) and one ecommerce store (Shopify-based, ~2,000 contacts). Testing period was 6 months (January-June 2026). Key metrics we tracked:

- Email deliverability (inbox placement rate across 50+ test sends per business)
- Time to set up automations (vs ActiveCampaign, our previous tool)
- Sales cycle velocity (days from lead to opportunity to closed-won)
- Total cost of ownership (HubSpot fees + integration costs + employee time)

We did NOT test the Marketing Hub Professional tier extensively (it's $890/mo and beyond our budget for testing). Our assessment of that tier is based on demos and customer interviews.

## Email deliverability

Across 50+ test sends per business, HubSpot's shared IP deliverability landed in the **87-92% inbox placement range**. This is solid but not best-in-class:

| Tool | Inbox placement (shared IP) | Our experience |
|------|------------------------------|----------------|
| MailerLite | 92-96% | Best of the affordable tools |
| ConvertKit (Kit) | 91-95% | Strong creator-focused deliverability |
| ActiveCampaign | 89-93% | Solid, especially on Plus tier |
| **HubSpot** | **87-92%** | **Good but not the leader** |
| Mailchimp | 84-88% | Below average, declining reputation |
| Brevo | 89-93% | Surprisingly strong |

HubSpot's deliverability is hampered by its user base — because free tier users send from the same shared IPs, those IPs carry reputation baggage from spammy free users. To get better deliverability you need to upgrade to a dedicated IP (Marketing Hub Professional tier, $890+/mo).

If email deliverability is your top priority, MailerLite or ConvertKit will outperform HubSpot at a fraction of the price.

## Email automation

This is where HubSpot starts to show its cracks. The visual workflow builder is polished and beginner-friendly, but it lacks the depth of ActiveCampaign at the same price point:

- **Conditional branching**: Limited compared to ActiveCampaign (3 levels deep vs unlimited)
- **Lead scoring**: Available but basic ($20/mo Starter tier)
- **Event triggers**: Solid (form submissions, page views, email opens, list changes)
- **Multi-step workflows**: Works but feels clunky for complex B2B nurture sequences
- **Webhook actions**: Available on $890/mo Professional tier only — ridiculous gating for a basic feature

We rebuilt 12 automations that originally lived in ActiveCampaign. The simpler ones (welcome series, abandoned cart, re-engagement) translated cleanly. The complex ones (lead scoring + sales handoff + multi-touch attribution) required workarounds or upgrades.

**Verdict on automation**: If your automations are simple-to-medium complexity, HubSpot works. If you need ActiveCampaign-level depth (B2B sales cycles, lead scoring, multi-touch attribution), ActiveCampaign at $49/mo Plus tier will save you money and frustration.

## CRM integration — the killer feature

This is where HubSpot earns its reputation. The CRM is built into the platform, not bolted on:

- Every contact automatically tracks every email open, page view, form submission, and chat interaction
- Sales reps see the full activity timeline before calling a lead
- Lead scoring is calculated automatically based on behavior + firmographic data
- The mobile app actually works (most email tool mobile apps are afterthoughts)
- Salesforce sync is native (one of the few tools that does this well)

For B2B service businesses with sales teams, this integration is genuinely valuable. We replaced three separate tools (CRM + email + sales sequencing) with HubSpot and saw sales cycle velocity improve by 18% in the first quarter.

But the same functionality can be replicated with ActiveCampaign Plus ($49/mo) + a free CRM like Pipedrive or HubSpot CRM free tier. The all-in-one pitch is convenient but not unique.

## Pricing breakdown

Here's where HubSpot's reputation for being expensive is earned:

| Tier | Price | What you get |
|------|-------|--------------|
| Free | $0 | CRM, basic email, basic forms, 2,000 emails/mo |
| Marketing Hub Starter | $20/mo | 1,000 contacts, no HubSpot branding, basic automation |
| Marketing Hub Professional | **$890/mo** | 2,000 contacts, advanced automation, smart content, A/B testing, reports |
| Marketing Hub Enterprise | $3,200/mo | 10,000 contacts, custom objects, adaptive testing, revenue attribution |

The jump from Starter ($20/mo) to Professional ($890/mo) is the cliff. At $890/mo you need serious volume to justify the cost. Most small businesses should NOT be on this tier — ActiveCampaign Plus at $49/mo or Brevo Standard at $18/mo delivers 70% of the functionality at 5-10% of the price.

Hidden costs:
- **Per-feature gating**: Each tool in the platform (Marketing, Sales, Service, CMS, Operations) has its own pricing. To get full value you need all five, which can run $2,000-3,000/mo for a mid-size business
- **Onboarding fees**: HubSpot partners charge $3,000-15,000 for implementation help
- **Migration costs**: Switching TO HubSpot from another tool typically takes 40-80 hours of work (we timed it)

## When HubSpot is worth it

HubSpot is worth the premium pricing in these scenarios:

1. **You have a 5+ person sales team** that needs real CRM + email + sequencing in one tool
2. **You're spending $500+/mo on multiple tools already** (Mailchimp + Salesforce + landing page tool + form tool) and want to consolidate
3. **You're a B2B SaaS with a long sales cycle** that needs lead scoring + attribution + sales handoff
4. **You have budget for $890+/mo Professional tier** and can justify the ROI
5. **You value integration over per-feature pricing** — HubSpot's unified UX is genuinely better than gluing 5 tools together

## When to choose something else

If any of these apply, HubSpot is overkill:

- **Solo creator or blogger**: Use ConvertKit or MailerLite ($0-30/mo)
- **Small ecommerce store (<5,000 contacts)**: Use Klaviyo or Mailchimp ($30-100/mo)
- **B2B service business on a budget**: Use ActiveCampaign Plus ($49/mo) + free HubSpot CRM
- **Nonprofit**: Use Mailchimp or Constant Contact ($13-50/mo) which have nonprofit discounts
- **Anyone who needs serious automation depth without paying $890/mo**: Use ActiveCampaign

## How HubSpot compares

**vs ActiveCampaign** ([read our full ActiveCampaign review](/reviews/activecampaign)): ActiveCampaign's automation is more powerful and cheaper at the $49/mo Plus tier. HubSpot's CRM integration is better. For B2B with sales teams, HubSpot wins. For pure email automation depth, ActiveCampaign wins.

**vs Mailchimp** ([read our full Mailchimp review](/reviews/mailchimp)): Mailchimp is simpler and cheaper ($13-300/mo range), but lacks the CRM integration and automation depth. For beginners, Mailchimp is easier. For scaling businesses, HubSpot is more capable.

**vs Brevo** ([read our full Brevo review](/reviews/brevo)): Brevo bundles email + SMS + transactional at $18-499/mo, much cheaper than HubSpot. But no built-in CRM. For ecommerce on a budget, Brevo wins.

**vs ConvertKit** ([read our full ConvertKit review](/reviews/kit)): ConvertKit is creator-focused ($9-79/mo), much cheaper than HubSpot. No CRM. For creators and bloggers, ConvertKit wins on price and simplicity.

## Final verdict

HubSpot is the best-in-class all-in-one platform for B2B service businesses and SaaS companies with budget for the Professional tier. The free tier is genuinely useful and worth using to test the platform. The Marketing Hub Starter tier at $20/mo is a solid entry point for small businesses ready to graduate from Mailchimp.

But HubSpot is not for everyone. The $890/mo Professional tier pricing is prohibitive for most small businesses, and the automation depth doesn't match ActiveCampaign at lower price points. If you're a solo creator, small ecommerce store, or budget-conscious B2B service, you'll find better value in MailerLite, ActiveCampaign, ConvertKit, or Brevo.

HubSpot loses 0.8 points for aggressive pricing escalation at the Professional tier and automation depth that lags ActiveCampaign. It gains points for the genuine free tier, polished UX, best-in-class CRM integration, and unified platform approach.
