ActiveCampaign's pricing is the deepest in the email marketing space — but also one of the most expensive entry points. After tracking 6 ActiveCampaign accounts from March to June 2026, including a real B2B consulting firm and a small SaaS company at $200K/year ARR, this breakdown shows what ActiveCampaign actually costs at every tier and when the premium automation earns its place.

The biggest difference between ActiveCampaign and other tools: **automation depth is the entire value proposition**, and ActiveCampaign gates that depth behind $49/mo Plus tier and especially $259/mo Professional tier. If you don't need the automation depth, ActiveCampaign is genuinely overpriced compared to MailerLite or Brevo.

## At a glance: 2026 ActiveCampaign pricing

| Plan | Price (monthly) | Subscriber cap | Key features |
|------|----------------|----------------|--------------|
| **Lite** | $29/mo (1K) | 1,000+ (scales linearly) | Email broadcasts, basic automations, signup forms, marketing CRM |
| **Plus** | $49/mo (1K) | 1,000+ | Advanced automations (visual canvas), lead scoring, sales CRM, site messages |
| **Professional** | $259/mo (1K) | 1,000+ | Predictive sending, advanced reporting, custom user roles, dedicated IP option |
| **Enterprise** | Custom ($1K+/mo) | 50K+ | SLA, custom contracts, dedicated success manager |

The **$49 to $259 jump** is the critical threshold — that's where predictive sending, advanced lead scoring, and dedicated IP availability enter. For most small businesses, the Lite → Plus jump captures the core value; Professional is genuinely out of budget unless you're at $200K+ ARR.

## Lite ($29/mo) — what's actually inside

ActiveCampaign's Lite tier is the most expensive entry point among mainstream tools — MailerLite Starter at $10/mo, Mailchimp Essentials at $13/mo, Brevo Lite at $9/mo, Klaviyo Free at $0 all undercut it. ActiveCampaign's $29/mo buys:

- Up to 1,000 contacts (scales linearly)
- Email broadcasts and newsletters
- Basic automations (linear workflows, no branches or conditional logic)
- Signup forms
- Marketing CRM (lightweight contact database)
- Drag-and-drop email designer
- 100+ integrations
- Email-only support

**What's NOT included on Lite**:
- Visual automation canvas (you get linear automations only, no drag-and-drop branching)
- Lead scoring
- Sales CRM (deal pipelines)
- Site messages / popups
- Conditional content (different email content based on subscriber attributes)
- Webhooks in automations
- SMS sending

For a small business that wants email + forms only, ActiveCampaign Lite is **genuinely overpriced** — MailerLite Starter at $10/mo delivers 80% of Lite's value for 1/3 the cost.

## Plus ($49/mo) — the real ActiveCampaign

The Plus tier is where ActiveCampaign becomes genuinely competitive. $49/mo buys:

- Everything in Lite
- **Visual automation canvas** with branches, conditional splits, and goals
- Advanced automations (long-running, multi-step workflows)
- Lead scoring (assign points to contact attributes, behaviors, email engagement)
- Sales CRM (deal pipelines, task management, win/loss tracking)
- Site messages and conditional content
- Webhooks in automations (connect to external services)
- SMS sending (additional cost per message)
- Phone + chat support (response within 4 hours)

For a **B2B service business** doing 50-200 leads per month, the Plus tier pays back through:
- Lead scoring → prioritize high-value leads → close rate improves 10-20%
- Deal pipelines → track deals through stages → forecast revenue accurately
- Conditional content → tailor emails to subscriber segment → open rates up 15-25%
- Visual automation canvas → automate 5-10 step sequences → save 5+ hours/week

**For a B2B consulting firm at $200K/year revenue, the $49/mo Plus tier pays back in <1 deal per quarter** based on improved close rate.

## Professional ($259/mo) — when it's worth the jump

The Professional tier is a significant jump from $49 to $259/mo — that's $210/mo additional cost. It's worth it for specific use cases:

- **Predictive sending**: AI-driven send time optimization (per-customer, machine learning model)
- **Advanced reporting**: custom reports, attribution, revenue tracking
- **Custom user roles**: granular team permissions (essential for 5+ person teams)
- **Dedicated IP option**: separate IP reputation (essential for high-volume senders 50K+/mo)
- **Migration support**: paid migration if moving 50K+ contacts

**For most small businesses, Professional is out of scope.** At $259/mo for 1K contacts, you're paying $0.26 per contact per month — way more than MailerLite's $0.01 per contact or Brevo's $9/mo flat. Professional is genuinely built for $200K+ ARR SaaS and agency clients.

## Enterprise ($1,000+/mo)

Enterprise is for very high-volume or specialized use cases:
- Custom SLA agreements
- Dedicated success manager
- Volume-based pricing (50K+ contacts)
- Custom contracts
- HIPAA compliance (medical, financial)
- Multi-account management (agencies with multiple clients)

For most businesses, **Enterprise is out of scope**. Mention only for context.

For most small businesses, the practical question is: **does the $40/mo premium for ActiveCampaign Plus vs MailerLite Advanced ($49 vs $20/mo) actually pay back**? The answer depends on whether you use the automation depth — B2B service businesses with lead scoring pipelines often say yes; ecommerce stores typically say no.

## B2B services vs ecommerce — where ActiveCampaign excels vs falls short

ActiveCampaign was **built for B2B services** (consulting, agencies, SaaS, professional services), not ecommerce. The platform architecture reflects that focus:

**ActiveCampaign excels at**:
- Lead scoring and grading
- Deal pipelines (CRM workflow)
- Multi-touch B2B sales sequences
- Webhook integrations to CRMs (Salesforce, HubSpot)
- Conditional content (personalization based on attribute)
- Complex branching automations
- Long-cycle sales automation (3-12 month nurture)

**ActiveCampaign falls short at**:
- Native Shopify integration (works, but not first-class like Klaviyo)
- Predictive lifetime value (Klaviyo has this, ActiveCampaign doesn't)
- SMS bundling (additional cost per SMS vs Klaviyo's bundling)
- Ecommerce-specific flows (abandoned cart, browse abandonment are weaker than Klaviyo)
- Visual product recommendations in email (Klaviyo has this, ActiveCampaign doesn't)

If you're a **B2B consulting firm or SaaS company at $100K+/year ARR**, ActiveCampaign Plus at $49-99/mo is genuinely worth it for the CRM + automation depth. If you're an **ecommerce store doing $100K-$500K/year**, Klaviyo at $60+/mo is the better pick.

## Transactional email — ActiveCampaign's gap

ActiveCampaign **does not include transactional email** in any tier. To send order confirmations, shipping notifications, or password resets, you need a separate service:

- SendGrid: $15-20/mo for 50K emails
- Mailgun: $35/mo
- Postmark: $15/mo
- Resend: pay-as-you-go
- AWS SES: ~$0.10 per 1,000 emails

For businesses doing both marketing + transactional from one vendor:
- **MailerLite + MailerSend**: $10/mo + $10/mo = $20/mo (cheapest bundle)
- **Brevo Lite**: $9/mo with transactional included (best bundle)
- **ActiveCampaign + Postmark**: $49/mo + $15/mo = $64/mo (3x more expensive)

If you need both marketing + transactional email, ActiveCampaign is significantly more expensive than the alternatives.

## When ActiveCampaign pricing is the right call

- **You're a B2B service business** (consulting, agency, SaaS) doing $100K+/year revenue
- **You need a marketing CRM + sales CRM** in one platform to avoid Salesforce/HubSpot costs ($25-90/user/mo)
- **You do multi-step, conditional automations** with branching and lead scoring
- **You can use phone + chat support** for onboarding
- **You have 1,000-10,000 subscribers** with predictable scaling needs

## When ActiveCampaign pricing is the wrong call

- **You have < 1,000 contacts** — MailerLite at $10/mo is 4x cheaper for similar core features
- **You're an ecommerce store** — Klaviyo's purpose-built ecommerce features pay back despite higher price
- **You want bundled transactional email** — Brevo's all-in-one is significantly cheaper
- **You're a creator selling courses or paid newsletters** — Kit's creator-specific features are more relevant
- **You don't use the automation depth** — basic automations on Lite are weaker than MailerLite or Mailchimp

## The verdict

ActiveCampaign's pricing is **the right call for B2B service businesses at $100K+/year revenue** that need deep automation and CRM in one platform. For other use cases (small businesses under $100K, ecommerce, creators), ActiveCampaign is **genuinely overpriced** compared to alternatives.

The biggest "watch out": **the $49/mo Plus tier is where ActiveCampaign becomes competitive — Lite at $29/mo lacks the visual automation canvas that defines the platform.** Don't sign up for Lite expecting Plus-like features.
