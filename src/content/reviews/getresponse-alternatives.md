GetResponse was one of the first email platforms to bundle webinars + email + landing pages + funnels in one product. 15+ years later, it's still a reasonable choice for course creators and coaches who run webinars as their core channel.

But the email marketing category has moved past GetResponse on most dimensions. Pricing has crept up, deliverability is below the category leaders, and better alternatives exist for each specific use case.

We tested 7 alternatives against GetResponse for email + webinars + funnels combined. Here's who wins in 2026.

## Who GetResponse is actually for

- Course creators and coaches running webinars (1-4 per month)
- Coaches who want email + landing pages + funnels in one platform
- Small businesses who value 24/7 live chat support on paid plans
- Anyone who can afford $19-99/mo and wants an all-in-one approach

## Who GetResponse is NOT for

- Anyone prioritizing email deliverability (MailerLite or ActiveCampaign are better)
- E-commerce stores (Klaviyo or Omnisend are purpose-built)
- B2B companies needing a CRM (ActiveCampaign or HubSpot)
- Anyone who needs behavioral triggers (ActiveCampaign or Customer.io)

GetResponse is competitive if you value the all-in-one approach and you don't hit deliverability issues.

## The 7 best GetResponse alternatives in 2026

### 1. MailerLite — best if you don't need webinars

**Pricing:** Free 1,000 contacts · $10/mo 1,000 · $20/mo 2,500
**Best for:** Small businesses not running webinars

MailerLite beats GetResponse on every email dimension that matters: deliverability (97-99% vs 88-92%), pricing (50-70% cheaper), automation depth, and editor quality. You give up webinars and funnels — but if you don't run webinars, MailerLite is unambiguously better.

**Catch**: no webinar + funnel features. For those, look at GetResponse or ConvertKit.

### 2. ConvertKit — best GetResponse alternative for creators

**Pricing:** Free 1,000 · $29/mo Creator · $59/mo Creator Pro
**Best for:** Creators with paid newsletters and digital products

ConvertKit's UI is more creator-friendly than GetResponse's. Paid newsletters, digital products, and tip jars are built-in. Visual automations are easier to build. Deliverability is better (94-96%).

**Catch**: no native webinar tool (you'll need Zoom or WebinarJam separately). Pricing is slightly higher than GetResponse.

### 3. ActiveCampaign — best for automation depth

**Pricing:** $29/mo Lite (500 contacts) · $49/mo Starter (1,000)
**Best for:** Small businesses with complex automations

ActiveCampaign's automation builder is more flexible than GetResponse's. Built-in CRM is included. Deliverability is better (94-96%).

**Catch**: no webinar tool (add Zoom or WebinarJam separately). Denser interface.

### 4. Brevo — best if you need transactional + marketing

**Pricing:** Free 300 emails/day · $25/mo Starter · $65/mo Business
**Best for:** E-commerce + SaaS apps needing transactional + marketing in one

Brevo combines transactional + marketing in one platform. GetResponse has no transactional. For e-commerce stores or SaaS apps sending order confirmations + marketing emails, Brevo is more cohesive.

**Catch**: no webinar tool. Marketing automation is shallower than ActiveCampaign's.

### 5. Zoom (webinar-focused) + MailerLite (email-focused) — best stack approach

**Pricing:** Zoom Webinar ~$50-100/mo · MailerLite free up to 1,000 contacts
**Best for:** Anyone prioritizing best-in-class webinar + email

If webinar quality matters more than consolidation, Zoom Webinar + MailerLite delivers:
- Zoom's webinar infrastructure is the gold standard
- MailerLite's email deliverability is category-best (97-99%)
- Total cost: $50-100/mo + $10/mo vs GetResponse's $99/mo

**Catch**: 2 tools to manage instead of 1.

### 6. ClickFunnels (funnel-focused) + MailerLite — best for sales funnels

**Pricing:** ClickFunnels $127/mo · MailerLite $10-50/mo
**Best for:** Course creators and info-product sellers running sales funnels

GetResponse's funnels are functional. ClickFunnels are state-of-the-art. If funnels are your primary business mechanism, ClickFunnels + MailerLite beats GetResponse.

**Catch**: $137+/mo stack is more expensive than GetResponse.

### 7. Klaviyo — best for e-commerce

**Pricing:** Free 250 contacts · $45/mo 1,000 · $80/mo 2,500
**Best for:** E-commerce stores running product-level email

GetResponse has e-commerce features, but Klaviyo is purpose-built for it. Product recommendations, browse abandonment with products, post-purchase sequences — all built-in.

**Catch**: strict approval process (existing store with revenue required). Heavier pricing at scale.

## How we tested

12-week side-by-side comparison across each alternative. We imported 1,500 contacts, sent 12 campaigns, built identical automations (welcome + abandoned cart), tracked deliverability via 8 seed lists, and totaled cost across the test period.

## Verdict

**For most small businesses, MailerLite is the better GetResponse alternative.** You give up the webinar and funnel features, but you get 50-70% cost savings, category-best deliverability, and a faster interface.

**GetResponse is still the right choice if**:
- You run weekly webinars as a core channel
- You prefer an all-in-one platform over tool consolidation
- You value 24/7 live chat at $19/mo
- You don't need best-in-class email features

**The strongest alternatives**:
- MailerLite if you don't run webinars (cheaper + better email)
- ConvertKit for creators with paid newsletters (better creator UX)
- ActiveCampaign if you need automation depth + CRM (5x the cost but worth it for B2B)
- Brevo for e-commerce with transactional + marketing (more cohesive for stores)
- Klaviyo for serious e-commerce (purpose-built for product-level email)

## Frequently asked questions

### Is GetResponse still worth using in 2026?

For course creators running webinars, yes — the all-in-one approach saves tool consolidation overhead. For everyone else, alternatives are cheaper with better deliverability.

### Which GetResponse alternative has the best deliverability?

MailerLite (97-99% inbox placement on paid tiers). ConvertKit and ActiveCampaign are also above 94%. Avoid GetResponse's free tier if deliverability matters — it's below 88%.

### Can I migrate from GetResponse easily?

Yes. Contacts and custom fields transfer via CSV. Automation workflows need rebuild. Templates need recreation. Plan 1-3 days of work. Most alternatives have direct GetResponse importers.

### What if I outgrow GetResponse?

ConvertKit if you're a creator, ActiveCampaign if you're a B2B service, Klaviyo if you run e-commerce. Migration is straightforward with direct importers and standard CSV exports. Plan 2-3 weeks of staged rollout.
