## The exact automations that drove the revenue

### 1. Welcome series (5 emails, 9 days)

The first email alone drove 23% of new-trial conversions. Here's the full sequence:

- **Email 1 (immediate)**: Welcome + 1 quick win (set up your first project in 5 minutes)
- **Email 2 (day 2)**: The 3 most common mistakes agencies make with project management
- **Email 3 (day 4)**: Case study — how a 12-person agency saved 11 hours/week
- **Email 4 (day 6)**: Free template (their best-performing lead magnet)
- **Email 5 (day 9)**: Trial offer (15% off first 3 months, no card required)

**Open rate**: 68% (industry average for B2B welcome: 50%)
**Click rate**: 14% (industry average: 5-8%)
**Conversion to paid**: 6.2% of welcome series completers

### 2. Trial-to-paid automation (4 emails, 14 days)

Triggered when a user signs up for the 14-day free trial:

- **Email 1 (day 2 of trial)**: "Here's what to set up first" — short, actionable
- **Email 2 (day 7 of trial)**: 50% complete nudge + case study
- **Email 3 (day 11 of trial)**: "Your trial ends in 3 days" + last-minute offer
- **Email 4 (day 13 of trial)**: "Trial ends tomorrow" + 30% off first 3 months

**Conversion rate**: 18.4% of trial users who received the series
**Revenue impact**: ~$3,200/month at peak

### 3. Educational weekly (1 email/week)

Every Wednesday at 9am local time, we sent a 600-word email on a specific topic:

- Topics: agency management, project workflows, client communication, pricing
- Format: 1 short lesson + 1 actionable takeaway
- CTA: 1 link to a relevant blog post or tool feature

**Open rate**: 41% (industry average for B2B weekly: 25-30%)
**Click rate**: 7.2% (industry average: 2-4%)
**Long-term LTV impact**: subscribers who received 8+ weekly emails had 3.4x higher LTV than trial-only users

### 4. Re-engagement (dormant subscriber win-back)

Triggered when a subscriber hasn't opened in 90 days:

- **Email 1**: "We miss you" + most popular content from the past 3 months
- **Email 2 (7 days later)**: "Should we stop emailing you?" with 1-click unsubscribe OR preference update
- **Tag**: "cold" — exclude from main newsletters but keep on automated sequences

**Re-engagement rate**: 12% of dormant subscribers came back
**List churn impact**: dropped monthly unsubscribe rate from 2.4% to 0.8%

### 5. Win-back (canceled users)

Triggered when a paying customer cancels:

- **Email 1 (7 days after cancel)**: "What went wrong?" survey (3 questions, multiple choice)
- **Email 2 (14 days after cancel)**: 30% off first 3 months if they return
- **Email 3 (30 days after cancel)**: Last email, no offer, just "we'd love to have you back"

**Win-back rate**: 4.2% of canceled users returned within 60 days

## What worked (and why)

**Three patterns drove 80% of the revenue**:

### 1. Behavior-triggered > time-triggered

The trial-to-paid series (behavior-triggered) had a 5.8x higher conversion rate than the educational weekly (time-triggered). The lesson: every email should fire based on a user action, not a calendar date.

### 2. Single CTA per email

Every email in every sequence had exactly one primary CTA. The welcome series Email 1 had one CTA: "Set up your first project." Not "read this, then check out our features, and consider upgrading." Just one action.

When we tested adding a secondary CTA, open rates stayed the same but click-through dropped 35%. Single CTA wins.

### 3. Specific subject lines > clever ones

"Here's what to set up first" (open rate 71%) beat "Your project awaits" (open rate 38%).

Specific subject lines consistently outperformed clever ones by 25-40%. The lesson: the reader is busy, they're scanning 50+ emails a day. Tell them exactly what the email will do for them.

## What didn't work (and what we'd change)

### 1. We added too many emails too fast

In month 6, we added a "tips & tricks" automation that fired every other day. Unsubscribe rate jumped from 0.8% to 1.6% in 2 weeks. We pulled it back to weekly and unsubscribe rate returned to normal.

**Lesson**: More emails ≠ more revenue. There's a real saturation point.

### 2. We didn't A/B test subject lines from the start

MailerLite's subject line A/B testing is built in. We used it starting month 7. Before that, we relied on gut feel. The tests consistently surprised us — the subject lines we thought would win lost by 20-40% open rate.

**Lesson**: A/B test every important send. Even if you think you know what works.

### 3. We waited too long to segment

The first 4 months, we sent the same emails to all subscribers. In month 5, we segmented by industry (agencies vs in-house teams) and saw a 28% lift in click-through for industry-specific content.

**Lesson**: Segment from day 1. It doesn't need to be complex — even 2 segments (industry A vs B) drives measurable lift.
