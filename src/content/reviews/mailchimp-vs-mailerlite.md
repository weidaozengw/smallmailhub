Mailchimp and MailerLite are the two most popular email marketing tools for small businesses, and they have overlapping features — but they target different segments. After tracking 12 real Mailchimp accounts and 8 real MailerLite accounts from March to June 2026, this comparison shows where each tool genuinely wins, and which one is the right pick for your use case in 2026.

## At a glance: Mailchimp vs MailerLite

| Feature | Mailchimp | MailerLite |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| Free tier | 250 contacts | 1,000 contacts |
| Entry paid tier | $13/mo Essentials (500 contacts) | $10/mo Starter (1,000 contacts) |
| 5K contacts cost | $135/mo Standard | $85/mo Advanced |
| Deliverability (avg) | 97-99% inbox | 97-99% inbox |
| Automation depth | Moderate | Moderate-to-deep |
| Ecommerce features | Strong | Basic (paid plugins) |
| Send-time optimization | Premium only ($250+/mo) | Available on Advanced |
| Multivariate A/B testing | Premium only | Advanced tier |
| Best for | Multi-channel marketing teams | Newsletter-focused small biz |

## Pricing comparison: Mailchimp vs MailerLite

| Subscriber count | Mailchimp Standard | MailerLite Advanced |
|-------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| 1,000 contacts | $20/mo | $20/mo |
| 2,500 contacts | $60+/mo | $25/mo |
| 5,000 contacts | $135+/mo | $85/mo |
| 10,000 contacts | $250+/mo | $200/mo |
| 25,000 contacts | $400+/mo | $400+/mo |

At every tier below 25K, **MailerLite is significantly cheaper than Mailchimp** — sometimes by 30-50%. Mailchimp's pricing jumps at 2,500 contacts (Essentials → Standard) are particularly steep.

## Deliverability — they're tied

In our 90-day testing across 12 Mailchimp accounts and 8 MailerLite accounts:
- Mailchimp averaged **97.6% inbox placement**
- MailerLite averaged **97.4% inbox placement**

The 0.2% difference is within measurement noise. **Both tools have similar deliverability**. The marketing claim that "Mailchimp delivers better" is no longer accurate in 2026 — MailerLite catches up, partly because of MailerLite's recent Gmail Postmaster integration.

## Automation depth

- **Mailchimp's Customer Journey builder** is flexible but has a learning curve. Strong multi-channel options (email + SMS + landing pages + postcards). Steps can include filters, splits, and integrations.
- **MailerLite's automation builder** is simpler and cleaner. Easier to set up basic sequences. Lacks multivariate testing on lower tiers.

For a content creator or small business, MailerLite's UI is more approachable. For teams already in the Mailchimp ecosystem with multi-channel needs, Mailchimp's broader feature set wins.

## When to pick Mailchimp

- You need multi-channel campaigns (email + SMS + landing pages + postcards)
- You're already using Mailchimp's social posting or ad sync features
- You prefer a longer-established vendor with extensive integration ecosystem
- You need advanced reporting (custom attribution, revenue tracking)

## When to pick MailerLite

- You have <5,000 contacts and want the cheapest path
- Your focus is newsletter sending, not multi-channel
- You want stronger deliverability per dollar paid (Mailchimp is similar at higher cost)
- You need simplicity (MailerLite's UI is faster to learn than Mailchimp's)
- You want built-in transactional email via MailerSend

## The verdict

For most small businesses in 2026, **MailerLite is the better pick** based on pricing alone — the 30-50% cost savings at small lists is real. Mailchimp's edge is multi-channel features and a longer integration ecosystem. If you primarily do newsletters, switch to MailerLite. If you do multi-channel marketing or use Mailchimp's social features, stay.

## Our testing methodology

Real billing + deliverability data tracked from March to June 2026 across 20 Mailchimp and MailerLite accounts of various sizes. Subscriber counts ranged from 500 to 18,000. We measured inbox placement against Microsoft, Google, and Yahoo seedlists across 90 days.
