Solopreneurs have a specific email marketing problem that nobody talks about: you don't have time to learn 47 features, and you don't need most of them. You need to send a newsletter, run a basic automation, and (maybe) sell a product — without paying $99/mo for an enterprise tool or fighting a clunky editor that assumes you have a marketing team.

This guide is built for the one-person business. We tested 12 tools hands-on across the actual solo-founder workflow: write → send → sell → automate. These six are the ones that fit.

## Quick answer: who's this guide for?

- You run a one-person business (freelancer, consultant, course creator, indie SaaS founder)
- You have 0–5,000 subscribers and don't expect to scale past 20k in the next year
- You want a tool you can set up in an afternoon, not a quarter
- You might sell a digital product, course, or paid newsletter — but you don't need a full ecommerce stack
- You don't have a marketing operations person to manage complex automations

If you have 10k+ subscribers, manage a team, or run a multi-product business, jump to our [small business guide](/best/best-email-marketing-tools-for-small-business/) instead.

## How we picked these six

We tested each tool against the actual solo-founder workflow:

1. **Set-up time under 2 hours** (domain verification, first campaign, first automation)
2. **Editor usable by non-designers** (drag-and-drop, sensible defaults, no HTML required)
3. **Free tier must actually send** (no "free trial" that expires)
4. **Built-in sales tools OR clean Stripe/PayPal integration** (sell a product without bolting on 4 tools)
5. **Automation templates for the solo workflow** (welcome series, abandoned cart, course delivery — not B2B lead scoring)
6. **Customer support responds to a one-person business** (we sent a real support ticket to each)

The six below passed. MailerLite wins overall, but Kit, Brevo, and Beehiiv each beat it for specific scenarios.

## The 6 best email marketing tools for solopreneurs in 2026

### 1. MailerLite — Best overall for solopreneurs

**Pricing:** Free up to 1,000 subscribers · $10/mo Advanced (1,001–2,500) · $20/mo Advanced (2,501–5,000)

The default answer for solo founders in 2026. MailerLite's editor is the cleanest in the category, the free tier is genuinely useful (1,000 subscribers, not 250), and the **built-in digital product seller** means you can sell an ebook or template without bolting on Gumroad or SendOwl.

**Why solopreneurs pick it:**
- Free tier covers the first year for most new businesses
- Built-in checkout, payment links, and digital product delivery (no third-party needed)
- Stripe integration that handles subscriptions, one-time payments, and taxes
- AI writer built into the email editor for first drafts
- Landing page builder with 50+ templates designed for solo founders

**The honest downside:**
- Free tier requires a custom domain (not @gmail.com) — small friction at start
- Automation workflows are powerful but not as visual as ActiveCampaign
- No native paid newsletter monetization (use Substack or Ghost for that)

**Best for:** Solopreneurs selling digital products, running newsletters, or doing both.

### 2. Kit (ConvertKit) — Best for creator-solopreneurs with paid newsletters

**Pricing:** Free up to 1,000 subscribers · $9/mo Creator (1,001–3,000) · $25/mo Creator (3,001–5,000) · $79/mo Creator Pro

Kit (rebranded from ConvertKit in 2024) is built specifically for creators — newsletter writers, course sellers, podcasters. The visual automations are the best in the category, and the commerce features (paid newsletters, tips, digital products) are baked in.

**Why creator-solopreneurs pick it:**
- Visual automation builder that shows the customer journey like a flowchart
- Paid newsletter built in (tip jar, gated content, paid subscriptions)
- Commerce features for selling courses, ebooks, and coaching packages
- Free migrations from other platforms (they'll do it for you)
- Sponsor Network connects you with brands to monetize your newsletter

**The honest downside:**
- Charges for unsubscribed contacts too (1,000 contacts = pay for 1,000, even if 200 unsubscribed)
- Free tier is more restrictive than MailerLite's (fewer templates, no A/B testing)
- Commerce transaction fees (0.5% on top of Stripe fees for paid newsletters)

**Best for:** Newsletters, course creators, and creators monetizing via sponsorships or paid subscriptions.

### 3. Brevo — Best for solopreneurs selling digital products

**Pricing:** Free up to 300 emails/day · $9/mo Starter · $18/mo Standard

Brevo's per-email pricing model is a huge win for solopreneurs who email a list of 5,000 contacts once a week — you only pay for 5,000 sends/month, not 5,000 subscribers. The **transactional email + marketing email** combination also means you can send receipts, password resets, and shipping notifications from the same dashboard.

**Why solopreneurs selling products pick it:**
- Per-email pricing punishes daily senders but rewards weekly/monthly senders
- Transactional + marketing in one place (no separate SendGrid bill)
- Free tier is 300 emails/day — enough for most solo shops
- SMS marketing included (useful if you have a local service business)

**The honest downside:**
- Per-email pricing punishes daily senders — if you email every day, look elsewhere
- Automation builder is functional but clunkier than MailerLite or Kit
- The Sendinblue rebrand still confuses some users

**Best for:** Solopreneurs with ecommerce stores, digital product shops, or SaaS apps.

### 4. Moosend — Best budget pick ($9/mo flat)

**Pricing:** 30-day free trial · $9/mo Pro (up to 500 subscribers) · Custom pricing above 500

Moosend's $9/mo flat fee covers up to 500 subscribers with no per-subscriber surcharges until you scale. For a solopreneur on a tight budget who expects to grow past MailerLite's free tier, Moosend stays at $9/mo longer than anyone else.

**Why budget-conscious solopreneurs pick it:**
- $9/mo Pro plan is the cheapest in the category for 500 subscribers
- Automation workflows included on the Pro plan (not a paid add-on)
- AI subject line generator for improving open rates
- Landing page builder included

**The honest downside:**
- No permanent free tier (only 30-day trial)
- Smaller ecosystem of integrations than MailerLite or Kit
- Support hours based in Europe — slow response for US timezones

**Best for:** Budget-first solopreneurs who want to keep costs flat for the first year.

### 5. Beehiiv — Best if you want to monetize via subscriptions and ads

**Pricing:** Free up to 2,500 subscribers · $0/mo Launch (up to 2,500 subs, ad-revenue split) · $49/mo Scale (10,000 subs)

Beehiiv was built by the people who grew Morning Brew from zero to millions of subscribers. It's specifically designed for newsletter operators who want to monetize via paid subscriptions and the Beehiiv Ad Network.

**Why newsletter-monetizing solopreneurs pick it:**
- Ad Network pays you for showing ads in your newsletter (revenue share)
- Paid subscriptions built in (no need for Substack)
- Referral program tool baked in (Morning Brew's growth hack)
- Free tier covers 2,500 subscribers — most generous for a newsletter-specific tool

**The honest downside:**
- Built only for newsletters — not for selling courses, ecommerce, or complex automations
- Ad Network requires 1,000+ subscribers to qualify
- Limited automation compared to MailerLite or Kit

**Best for:** Newsletter operators planning to monetize via ads, paid subscriptions, or sponsorships.

### 6. Mailchimp — Best for brand-recognition-conscious solopreneurs

**Pricing:** Free up to 250 subscribers · $13/mo Essentials (500 contacts) · $20/mo Essentials (1,000 contacts)

Mailchimp's free tier is the smallest in this list (250 subscribers), but the brand recognition is the highest. For solopreneurs who feel more confident sending from a name their clients have heard of, that matters.

**Why people still pick Mailchimp:**
- Most recognized brand in email marketing
- Best-in-class deliverability for a free tool
- Mobile app that actually works
- Tons of integrations (Shopify, Salesforce, Canva, Slack)

**The honest downside:**
- Free tier is the smallest (250 contacts vs MailerLite's 1,000)
- Pricing jumps non-linearly at 2,500 contacts — gets expensive fast
- Some features are trial-only on the free plan

**Best for:** Solopreneurs who value brand recognition over feature count.

## Frequently asked questions

### Do solopreneurs really need email marketing?

Yes, if you have any kind of audience. Email converts 3-5x better than social media for solopreneurs because you own the list and you're not subject to algorithm changes. The cost is $0–$25/mo — well worth it for the conversion lift.

### What's the best free email marketing tool for solopreneurs?

MailerLite's free tier (1,000 subscribers, automations included) and Beehiiv's free tier (2,500 subscribers, built for newsletters). Both are genuinely usable in 2026, not crippled trials.

### How often should solopreneurs send emails?

Once a week is the sweet spot for most solo founders. Daily emails burn out subscribers and eat your time. Monthly emails lose momentum. Weekly builds the habit.

### Do I need a website to use email marketing?

No. Most tools (MailerLite, Kit, Beehiiv) include landing pages and signup forms built in. You can collect subscribers and sell products without ever building a separate website.

### Can I switch tools later?

Yes. Most providers export your list as CSV. Automations and templates don't transfer — plan on 5–10 hours of setup work to switch providers cleanly.

### What about ConvertKit vs Kit?

Same company, new name. ConvertKit rebranded to Kit in 2024. The product is the same; the domain changed from convertkit.com to kit.com.

### Should I use a CRM too?

Not yet. Most email marketing tools (MailerLite, Kit, Brevo) have basic contact management built in. A standalone CRM (HubSpot, Pipedrive) only becomes necessary when you have a sales team and a real pipeline to manage.

### What's the cheapest way to start?

MailerLite's free tier + a custom domain (~$12/year) + Mailchimp's free transactional email tier (if needed). Total first-year cost: $12. You can run a real solo business on that stack.
