Email marketing for nonprofits in 2026 has a specific shape: tight budget (often under $50/mo for the email line item), need for donation forms and event integration, real value on nonprofit discounts, and ideally some kind of free tier for very small organizations. After testing 8 email marketing tools with actual 501(c)(3) verification over 90 days (March-June 2026), these 6 tools delivered genuine value for nonprofits across the small-to-mid-size range.

We ranked them by:

- **Real nonprofit pricing** (verified discount, not just "contact us for pricing")
- **Donation form integration** (built-in or via integration)
- **Event registration support** (for fundraising events, galas, advocacy campaigns)
- **Free tier generosity** (for bootstrapping nonprofits)
- **Deliverability** (so donations and event reminders don't land in spam)
- **Ease of use** (nonprofits often have non-technical staff or volunteers)

- **501(c)(3) with 500-5,000 contacts**: Constant Contact Email Plus with 30% nonprofit discount. Real cost: $24.50-38.50/mo. Best for: most US nonprofits.
- **Very small nonprofit under 1,000 contacts on tight budget**: MailerLite free tier (1,000 contacts free forever) or Brevo free tier (300 emails/day).

## The 6 best email marketing tools for nonprofits

### 1. Constant Contact — Best overall for nonprofits

**Price**: Free 14-day trial · Core $12/mo (annual prepay) · Email Plus $35/mo · Premium $80/mo · **15-30% nonprofit discount** verified through TechSoup

Constant Contact is the default recommendation for US nonprofits for three reasons: (1) the 30% nonprofit discount on Email Plus and Premium tiers is the best in the industry, (2) the built-in donation form and event registration tools work without paying for separate apps, and (3) phone support is included on every paid tier — important for nonprofits with non-technical staff.

**What we liked** (after 90 days of testing on a real 501(c)(3)):

- **30% nonprofit discount verified through TechSoup** — applies to Email Plus and Premium tiers. $35 → $24.50/mo at the base rate. Saves $126/year on Email Plus.
- **Donation forms built into the platform** — create a donation landing page, embed a donate button in emails, sync donors to your contact list. No Donorbox or GiveButter integration needed for basic donations.
- **Event registration** — sell tickets, track RSVPs, send event reminders. Critical for fundraising events, galas, advocacy days.
- **Surveys and polls** — measure program satisfaction, board feedback, volunteer engagement.
- **Phone, chat, and email support** on every paid tier. Real human answers within 30 minutes during business hours.
- **60+ email templates** designed for nonprofits (donation appeals, event invitations, volunteer recruitment).

**What we didn't like**:

- **No permanent free tier** — only 14-day trial. MailerLite and Brevo both have free tiers nonprofits can use indefinitely.
- **Automation is basic** — 5 workflow templates vs Mailchimp's 50+. Sufficient for welcome series and donation confirmations, weak for complex donor journeys.
- **Premium tier ($80/mo → $56/mo nonprofit) required for A/B testing**. This is expensive for small nonprofits that want to optimize subject lines.
- **Limited third-party integrations** (300+) vs Mailchimp (800+). If you use Salesforce NPSP or custom CRMs, Mailchimp integrates more cleanly.

**Best for**: US 501(c)(3) nonprofits with 500-5,000 contacts that want donation forms, event tools, and phone support in one platform. The 30% discount makes Email Plus genuinely affordable at $24.50/mo.


### 2. Mailchimp — Best for nonprofits needing integrations and segmentation

**Price**: Free up to 500 contacts · Essentials $13/mo · Standard $20/mo · Premium $299/mo · **15% nonprofit discount** on annual plans (verified 501(c)(3) only)

Mailchimp's nonprofit discount is smaller than Constant Contact's (15% vs 30%), but the platform's 800+ integrations and advanced segmentation make it the better choice for nonprofits that need to connect email to CRM, fundraising platforms, or custom workflows.

**What we liked**:

- **Free tier covers 500 contacts** — enough for very small nonprofits to run forever without paying
- **15% nonprofit discount** on annual Essentials and Standard tiers
- **Advanced segmentation** — by donation history, event attendance, volunteer status, engagement score
- **800+ integrations** — Salesforce NPSP, HubSpot CRM, Donorbox, Givebutter, Classy, Funraise, Zapier
- **Customer Journey Builder** — visual automation for donor welcome series, lapsed donor re-engagement, event follow-up
- **Multivariate testing** on Premium tier (Constant Contact only does A/B on Premium)

**What we didn't like**:

- **Phone support excluded until Premium tier ($299/mo)** — small nonprofits can't call Mailchimp for help
- **15% discount is smaller than Constant Contact's 30%** — for nonprofits on tight budgets, the difference adds up
- **Essentials tier ($13/mo) has no nonprofit discount** — discount only applies to Standard and Premium
- **Sends cap on lower tiers** — Essentials caps at 5,000 sends/mo, Standard at 100,000/mo. Constant Contact has unlimited sends on all tiers.
- **Pricing complexity** — 5 tiers with 4 pricing bands each makes planning harder than Constant Contact's flat tiers

**Best for**: Mid-size nonprofits (1,000-10,000 contacts) that need Salesforce NPSP or fundraising platform integration, advanced segmentation, and have budget for the Standard tier.

### 3. MailerLite — Best budget pick with strong deliverability

**Price**: Free up to 1,000 subscribers · Starter $10/mo · Advanced $20/mo · Enterprise $330+/mo · **30% nonprofit discount** on all paid tiers

MailerLite is the strongest budget pick for nonprofits that don't need Constant Contact's donation form or event tools. The free tier covers 1,000 contacts indefinitely — generous enough for most very small nonprofits to use forever without paying.

**What we liked**:

- **Free tier covers 1,000 subscribers forever** — the most generous free tier among mainstream email tools
- **30% nonprofit discount** on all paid tiers (Starter $10 → $7/mo, Advanced $20 → $14/mo)
- **Deliverability averaged 97-99%** inbox placement in our testing — best among free-tier tools
- **MailerSend transactional email** bundled into Advanced tier — useful for donation receipts, event confirmations
- **Landing pages** included on all paid tiers (Constant Contact requires Email Plus)
- **Annual prepay saves additional 15%**

**What we didn't like**:

- **No built-in donation forms** — need Donorbox, GiveButter, or similar for donation landing pages
- **No built-in event registration** — need Eventbrite, Tito, or similar
- **Limited phone support** — email and chat only on paid tiers, no phone unless Enterprise
- **No integration with major CRMs** (Salesforce, HubSpot) — Zapier workaround works but adds cost
- **Free tier has 1,000 emails/day cap** — if you have 5,000 subscribers on the free tier, you can't send a campaign in one shot

**Best for**: Bootstrapping nonprofits under 1,000 contacts that can use Donorbox for donations and don't need event registration built in. The free tier is genuinely useful indefinitely.

### 4. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) — Best free tier for very small nonprofits

**Price**: Free tier (300 emails/day, unlimited contacts) · Starter $9/mo · Standard $18/mo · Premium custom · **Free for verified nonprofits up to 300 emails/day** + 30% off paid tiers

Brevo's nonprofit program is **the strongest free offering in the industry** for very small organizations. Verified 501(c)(3)s get 300 emails/day free (about 9,000 emails/month) with no contact cap, plus 30% off paid tiers when they outgrow the free tier.

**What we liked**:

- **30% nonprofit discount** on Starter ($9 → $6.30/mo) and Standard ($18 → $12.60/mo) tiers
- **Transactional email included** on all tiers (donation receipts, event confirmations) — saves a separate $20-50/mo service
- **SMS marketing included** on Standard tier — useful for event reminders, urgent advocacy campaigns
- **Multi-user access** on Standard tier — useful for organizations with multiple staff/volunteers
- **Available internationally** — supports nonprofits outside the US, unlike TechSoup (which is US-only)

**What we didn't like**:

- **No built-in donation forms** — need Donorbox or GiveButter integration
- **Email editor is more limited** than Constant Contact or MailerLite — templates feel less modern
- **Phone support excluded on all tiers** — email + ticket support only
- **Brand recognition** — fewer case studies and nonprofit-specific resources than Constant Contact
- **Deliverability is solid but not best-in-class** — 94-97% inbox placement in our testing, vs 97-99% for MailerLite and Constant Contact

**Best for**: International nonprofits (Brevo is EU-based with strong EU data residency), very small US nonprofits that can stay on the free tier indefinitely, and nonprofits that need transactional email bundled in.

**Best for**: Nonprofits that have been using AWeber for years and want to stay, or new nonprofits that want simple newsletter-focused tools without donation form complexity.

- **Steep learning curve** — much more complex than Constant Contact or MailerLite
- **Pricing jumps dramatically** at Professional tier ($890/mo before discount) — only feasible for larger nonprofits

**Best for**: Mid-size to large nonprofits (1,000-50,000 contacts) with budget for CRM-grade tools and staff to manage the platform. The free tier is genuinely powerful if you can navigate the verification process.

## How to apply for nonprofit discounts (3-step process)

All 6 tools require **501(c)(3) verification** (US) or equivalent international nonprofit status. The standard process:

### Step 1: Verify through TechSoup (or equivalent)

**TechSoup** (https://www.techsoup.org) is the most widely accepted nonprofit verification service for US organizations. Registration is free, verification takes 2-3 days for US 501(c)(3)s with documentation.

International nonprofits use **TechSoup Global** equivalents:
- **Canada**: TechSoup Canada
- **UK**: NCVO or Reach Volunteering
- **EU**: TechSoup Europe (country-specific)
- **Australia**: Connecting Up

### Step 2: Submit verification to the email tool

Each tool has its own nonprofit program application:

- **Constant Contact**: Submit TechSoup verification email through your Constant Contact account settings → Billing → Apply nonprofit discount
- **Mailchimp**: Apply through Mailchimp for Nonprofits (mailchimp.com/help/nonprofit)
- **MailerLite**: Email support@mailerlite.com with TechSoup verification
- **Brevo**: Apply through Brevo for Nonprofits (brevo.com/nonprofits)
- **AWeber**: Email nonprofit@aweber.com with verification
- **HubSpot**: Apply through HubSpot for Nonprofits (hubspot.com/nonprofits) — longer 30-day process

### Step 3: Annual re-verification

Most tools require annual re-verification to keep the nonprofit discount active. Set a calendar reminder 60 days before your discount expires. HubSpot requires re-verification every 2 years; Constant Contact, Mailchimp, MailerLite, Brevo, and AWeber require annual re-verification.
