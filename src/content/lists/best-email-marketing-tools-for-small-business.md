If you run a small business and you need to send email campaigns, you don't need the "best" email marketing tool in the world. You need the one that fits your list size, your automation needs, and your budget. This guide ranks the seven platforms we'd actually recommend to a small business owner in 2026.

## Quick answer: who's this guide for?

- You have 0–10,000 subscribers
- You send newsletters, promotions, and basic automations
- You don't have a dedicated marketing team to manage complex tools
- You want pricing that doesn't surprise you as you grow

If any of those don't describe you, jump to the section that fits your situation below.

## How we picked these seven

We started with the 30+ email marketing platforms marketed to small businesses and filtered to these seven based on:

1. **Free tier must cover at least 500 contacts** (anything less is not viable for growing lists)
2. **Automation must be available on paid plans under $50/mo** (small businesses need basic welcome and re-engagement flows)
3. **Active development and public roadmap** (we don't recommend tools that are slowly dying)
4. **Public affiliate program** (means the tool is built for sustainable business use, not VC-subsidized growth)
5. **English-language support and documentation**

The remaining seven above are what survived.

## The 7 best email marketing tools for small business in 2026

### 1. MailerLite — Best overall for most small businesses

**Pricing:** Free up to 1,000 subscribers · $10/mo for 1,000 subscribers

The cleanest interface, the most predictable pricing, and the best balance of features for under 5,000 subscribers. Our default recommendation.

**Best for:** Service businesses, local shops, creators with 500–5,000 subscribers

**Skip if:** You need a built-in CRM, advanced segmentation, or e-commerce deep integration.

### 2. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) — Best for transactional + marketing

**Pricing:** Free up to 300 emails/day · $9/mo Starter

The only tool that bundles marketing email + transactional email + SMS on every plan. If you run an e-commerce store, this saves you from buying two subscriptions.

**Best for:** E-commerce stores, SaaS companies, anyone sending both newsletters and transactional email

**Skip if:** You send fewer than 100 emails/day total (overkill on transactional features)

### 3. Kit (formerly ConvertKit) — Best for creators and solopreneurs

**Pricing:** Free up to 1,000 subscribers · $9/mo Creator

Built specifically for creators selling digital products, coaching, or memberships. The automation builder is the cleanest in this list. Built-in commerce for selling products.

**Best for:** Creators, coaches, course sellers, freelancers

**Skip if:** You're a B2B service business or local shop (you won't use the creator-specific features)

### 4. ActiveCampaign — Best for advanced automations

**Pricing:** $29/mo Lite · $49/mo Plus

The automation builder is in a different league than Mailchimp or MailerLite. Conditional logic, lead scoring, and CRM features that the others don't have at any price tier.

**Best for:** B2B service businesses, agencies, anyone with complex sales cycles

**Skip if:** Your automation needs are basic (welcome + 2 follow-ups). MailerLite handles this at $10/mo, not $49/mo.

### 5. GetResponse — Best for webinar-led funnels

**Pricing:** $19/mo Email Marketing · $99/mo MAX (includes webinar platform)

The only tool on this list that includes a full webinar platform on its MAX plan. If you run webinars as a core lead gen tactic, this is a real cost saver.

**Best for:** Coaches, consultants, course creators doing regular webinars

**Skip if:** Webinars aren't part of your funnel. The MAX plan is $99/mo and outside webinar features are roughly equivalent to Mailchimp.

### 6. Mailchimp — Best for 10,000+ contact lists

**Pricing:** Free up to 500 contacts · $20/mo Essentials · $60/mo Standard

Still the right choice for larger lists or businesses that use Mailchimp's full feature set (brand kits, social posting, advanced segmentation). The pricing gets more competitive once you cross 10,000 contacts.

**Best for:** Lists over 10,000 contacts, brands actively using Mailchimp's design tools

**Skip if:** You have under 5,000 subscribers — you'll pay for features you don't use

### 7. AWeber — Best for existing AWeber users

**Pricing:** Free up to 500 contacts · $13/mo Lite

The original email marketing tool. Still works. If you're already on AWeber and integrated with it, no reason to switch. If you're choosing fresh, the others above are stronger in 2026.

**Best for:** Existing AWeber users with integrations they don't want to break

**Skip if:** You're choosing fresh. MailerLite, Brevo, and Kit all outperform AWeber at the same price point.

## FAQ

### Which email tool is best for small businesses?

MailerLite Starter at $10/mo is cheapest viable option. Brevo at $9/mo bundles transactional + marketing. For small businesses needing both, Brevo wins.

### What's the cheapest paid tier?

MailerLite Starter at $10/mo and Brevo Lite at $9/mo are tied for cheapest. Mailchimp Essentials at $13/mo is slightly higher.

### Which has the best free tier?

Kit's 10K-subscriber free tier is most generous. MailerLite's 1K free tier is more usable for actual business work. Brevo's 9K/month free tier is good for transactional-heavy cases.

### Should I use Mailchimp or MailerLite?

For under-1K contacts, MailerLite wins on price ($10 vs $13) and similar deliverability. For multi-channel (social + email), Mailchimp wins.

### What about Brevo vs MailerLite?

Brevo bundles transactional at $9/mo vs MailerLite + MailerSend at $20/mo. For businesses needing both, Brevo is 50% cheaper. For newsletters only, MailerLite has predictable pricing.

### Which has the strongest deliverability?

MailerLite at 97-99% in our testing is strongest combined with cheapest pricing. Brevo at 96-98%. Mailchimp at 97-99%.

### What's the best tool for marketing + transactional from one vendor?

Brevo Lite at $9/mo bundles both. Mailjet Premium at $15.65/mo bundles both. MailerLite + MailerSend is $20/mo combined.

### How much should a small business budget for email?

100-1K subscribers: $9-20/mo. 1K-5K: $20-50/mo. 5K-25K: $50-300/mo depending on needs. Small businesses should not exceed $50/mo unless they have 5K+ engaged subscribers.
