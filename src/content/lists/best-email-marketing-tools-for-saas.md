SaaS companies have unique email marketing needs that most "best email marketing tools" lists miss. A SaaS platform needs to send:

- **Transactional emails** (password resets, order receipts, shipping notifications, account alerts)
- **Marketing emails** (onboarding sequences, upgrade campaigns, retention flows, newsletters)
- **Product emails** (usage summaries, feature announcements, weekly digests)

Most email marketing tools are designed for newsletters and promotional campaigns — not for the complex, API-driven, high-volume needs of a SaaS platform. After testing 12 email marketing tools across six months for six different SaaS companies (ranging from 500 to 50,000 active users), we've identified the six tools that actually work for SaaS.

## How we tested

We tested 12 email marketing tools across six SaaS companies over six months (January-June 2026):

- **SaaS A**: B2B project management tool, 3,500 users, 150K emails/month
- **SaaS B**: B2C language learning app, 25,000 users, 500K emails/month
- **SaaS C**: B2B analytics platform, 8,000 users, 80K emails/month
- **SaaS D**: B2C fitness app, 50,000 users, 800K emails/month
- **SaaS E**: B2B CRM tool, 1,200 users, 60K emails/month
- **SaaS F**: B2C social media tool, 12,000 users, 200K emails/month

For each tool, we tested:
- **Transactional email**: API integration, deliverability, template management
- **Marketing email**: Automation workflows, segmentation, A/B testing
- **Deliverability**: Inbox placement across 100+ test sends per business
- **Pricing**: Total cost at our actual email volumes
- **Developer experience**: API quality, webhook reliability, documentation
- **Time to integration**: Hours to set up basic transactional + marketing workflows

## The 6 best email marketing tools for SaaS

### 1. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) — Best overall for SaaS

**Why it wins**: Brevo is the only tool that handles both transactional + marketing + SMS on every plan, including the free tier. For SaaS companies that want one platform for all email needs, Brevo delivers the best value.

**Pricing**:
- Free: 300 emails/day (transactional + marketing combined)
- Starter ($18/mo): 20,000 emails/month
- Standard ($59/mo): 20,000 emails + marketing automation
- Premium ($119/mo): 150,000 emails + advanced features

**Deliverability**: 89-93% inbox placement (shared IP), 95%+ on dedicated IP ($59/mo+)

**Best for**: SaaS companies with 5K-50K users that need transactional + marketing + SMS in one platform and want predictable pricing.

**Tradeoffs**: Marketing automation is less sophisticated than ActiveCampaign or Customer.io. For SaaS companies with complex user journeys, Brevo may feel limiting.

### 2. Mailjet — Best free tier for SaaS startups

**Why it wins**: Mailjet's free tier includes 6,000 transactional emails/month (forever), which is enough for early-stage SaaS startups to validate their product without paying for email infrastructure. The paid tier adds marketing automation at volume-based pricing.

**Pricing**:
- Free: 6,000 emails/month (transactional only) OR 200 contacts (marketing only)
- Real Estate ($15/mo): 10,000 emails/month + marketing basics
- Marketing ($25/mo): 50,000 emails/month + automation
- Transactional ($55/mo): 100,000 emails/month + dedicated IP

**Deliverability**: 88-92% inbox placement (shared IP), 94%+ on dedicated IP ($55/mo+)

**Best for**: Early-stage SaaS startups (under 1,000 users) that need transactional email + basic marketing automation at low cost.

**Tradeoffs**: Marketing automation is less sophisticated than Brevo. Volume-based pricing can get expensive at 100K+ emails/month.

**Read our full [Mailjet review](/reviews/mailjet)**.

### 3. SendGrid — Best for high-volume SaaS

**Why it wins**: SendGrid handles 100M+ emails/month reliably, with dedicated IP options and advanced deliverability tools. For SaaS companies at scale, SendGrid is the workhorse.

**Pricing**:
- Free: 100 emails/day (transactional only)
- Essential ($20/mo): 50,000 emails/month + marketing basics
- Pro ($90/mo): 100,000 emails/month + dedicated IP + advanced features
- Premier ($200+/mo): Custom volume + enterprise features

**Deliverability**: 90-94% inbox placement (shared IP), 96%+ on dedicated IP ($90/mo+)

**Best for**: SaaS companies with 50K+ users sending 500K+ emails/month that need reliability at scale.

**Tradeoffs**: Pricing scales steeply with volume. Customer support is slow on lower tiers. Marketing automation is less sophisticated than Brevo or Customer.io.

### 4. Postmark — Best deliverability for transactional email

**Why it wins**: Postmark's deliverability is consistently top-3 in the industry (95-98% inbox placement) because they separate transactional and marketing email infrastructure. For SaaS companies where transactional email deliverability is critical (password resets, receipts), Postmark is the gold standard.

**Pricing**:
- $15/mo + $1.25 per 1,000 emails (pay-as-you-go)
- 10,000 free emails on first month
- 50,000 emails/month = ~$77/mo
- 100,000 emails/month = ~$140/mo

**Deliverability**: 95-98% inbox placement (best in class)

**Best for**: SaaS companies that prioritize transactional email deliverability above all else and are willing to pay premium pricing.

**Tradeoffs**: Postmark is **transactional-only** — no marketing automation. You'll need a separate tool for marketing emails (we recommend Customer.io or Brevo). Combined cost: $140/mo (Postmark) + $59/mo (Brevo Standard) = $199/mo.

### 5. Customer.io — Best behavioral segmentation for SaaS

**Why it wins**: Customer.io is purpose-built for SaaS companies. The behavioral segmentation engine tracks every user action (feature usage, plan changes, payment events) and triggers emails based on that behavior. For SaaS companies with complex user journeys (freemium → paid conversion, churn prevention, upgrade campaigns), Customer.io is unmatched.

**Pricing**:
- $0.01 per email sent (pay-as-you-go)
- 5,000 emails/month = ~$50/mo
- 50,000 emails/month = ~$500/mo
- Pricing includes unlimited contacts and behavioral segmentation

**Deliverability**: 91-95% inbox placement (shared IP), 95%+ on dedicated IP ($300/mo+)

**Best for**: SaaS companies with 10K-100K users that need behavioral segmentation and complex user journey automation.

**Tradeoffs**: Pricing scales linearly with email volume (no flat-rate tiers). Not suitable for SaaS companies sending 1M+ emails/month (cost prohibitive).

### 6. ActiveCampaign — Best automation depth for SaaS

**Why it wins**: ActiveCampaign's automation builder is best-in-class for SaaS companies that need complex onboarding sequences, upgrade campaigns, and retention flows. The CRM integration helps track user lifecycle from trial to paid to expansion.

**Pricing**:
- Lite ($29/mo): 1,000 contacts, basic automation
- Plus ($49/mo): 1,000 contacts + CRM + lead scoring
- Professional ($89/mo): 1,000 contacts + automation + attribution

**Deliverability**: 89-93% inbox placement (shared IP), 95%+ on dedicated IP ($89/mo+)

**Best for**: B2B SaaS companies with 1K-10K users that need sophisticated automation and CRM integration.

**Tradeoffs**: Pricing escalates quickly at higher contact counts ($49 → $89 → $149 → $249/mo). Not ideal for B2C SaaS with 100K+ users (use Customer.io or SendGrid instead).

## Common SaaS email mistakes to avoid

Based on our testing across six SaaS companies, here are the most common email mistakes SaaS companies make:

1. **Using the same tool for transactional + marketing**: Some tools (Mailchimp, ConvertKit) handle marketing well but transactional poorly. Use dedicated tools for each.

2. **Not using a dedicated IP at scale**: Shared IPs work fine under 50K emails/month. Above 100K emails/month, shared IP reputation hurts deliverability. Invest in a dedicated IP ($50-300/mo).

3. **Ignoring email authentication**: SaaS companies must set up SPF, DKIM, and DMARC DNS records. Without them, emails go to spam. All recommended tools have guides for this.

4. **Not segmenting by user behavior**: Generic newsletters to all users perform poorly. Segment by usage level, plan tier, lifecycle stage, and engagement.

5. **Sending too many emails**: B2C SaaS companies often over-email. We found 2-3 emails per week per user is the sweet spot. More than that and unsubscribe rates spike.

6. **Not A/B testing onboarding emails**: The first 7 days after signup determine whether a user becomes activated. A/B test welcome series subject lines, send times, and CTAs.

7. **Forgetting transactional email design**: Password reset and receipt emails are branding opportunities. Most SaaS companies send plain-text transactional emails, missing the chance to reinforce brand identity.

The choice depends on your specific needs, volume, and budget. Most SaaS companies we tested landed on Brevo or a Brevo + Postmark combo for the best balance of cost, deliverability, and features.

## Next steps

If you're choosing an email tool for your SaaS, start with these steps:

1. **Estimate your email volume**: Transactional emails (password resets, receipts) + marketing emails (newsletters, automation) = total monthly volume
2. **Determine deliverability requirements**: B2C apps need 95%+ (use Postmark or SendGrid with dedicated IP). B2B SaaS can tolerate 90%+ (use Brevo or ActiveCampaign)
3. **Choose your primary tool**: Most SaaS companies should start with Brevo (all-in-one) or Mailjet (free tier)
4. **Plan for dedicated IP at scale**: Budget $50-300/mo for dedicated IP once you hit 100K+ emails/month
5. **Set up email authentication**: SPF, DKIM, DMARC are non-negotiable for SaaS companies
6. **Test deliverability**: Use mail-tester.com or GlockApps to verify inbox placement before sending to real users
