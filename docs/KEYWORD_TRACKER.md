# Keyword Ranking Tracker

After site is live + GSC verified, use this CSV to track keyword rankings weekly.

## Setup

1. In Google Search Console → Performance
2. Filter by date range: Last 28 days (or your preferred window)
3. Click "Export" → Download CSV
4. Open in Numbers/Excel/Sheets
5. Filter for queries containing your target keywords (column "Top queries")
6. Copy data into this tracker

## Tracker columns

## Weekly check-in questions

Every Monday morning, answer these questions:

1. **Which pages gained rankings?** (Look for pages with position changes of -2 or more, meaning they moved up)
2. **Which pages lost rankings?** (Look for pages with position changes of +2 or more)
3. **Which new queries appeared?** (Look for queries you weren't tracking before)
4. **Which pages have high impressions but low CTR?** (These need title/description tweaks)
5. **Which pages have clicks but no conversions?** (Need to check affiliate link placement)

## Action triggers

- **Position 1-3:** Maintain. Update the page quarterly with fresh stats. Don't over-optimize.
- **Position 4-10:** Push to top 3. Add internal links from newer pages, update the content with current year references.
- **Position 11-20:** Major refresh needed. Look at top 3 ranking pages for the keyword — what are they doing that you're not?
- **Position 21+:** Reassess keyword targeting. Either rewrite from scratch or pivot to a different keyword.

## When to add a new page

Add a new alternatives page when:
- A search query for `[tool] alternatives` has 100+ monthly searches (Google Trends shows consistent volume)
- AND you can write a 2,000+ word honest comparison
- AND there's no obvious winner in the top 3 results (meaning the niche is still open)

Add a new review when:
- The tool has a public affiliate program (you can earn from linking)
- AND the tool has 1,000+ monthly searches for `[tool] review` or `[tool] pricing`
- AND the tool isn't already on your alternatives pages

## What to ignore

Don't track:
- Brand searches (searches for "MailPilot" — irrelevant until you have brand recognition)
- Informational queries (people asking "what is email marketing")
- Queries with 0 impressions (won't rank anytime soon)

Focus on:
- Buyer intent queries ("best X", "X alternatives", "X pricing", "X review")
- Queries where you rank 4-30 (push to top 3)
